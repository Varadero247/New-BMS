---

## Facilitator Energy Note

HSE professionals are emotionally invested in their domain — safety failures have real human consequences. This group tends to arrive with urgency and genuine engagement. The incident recording exercises may surface difficult real-world experiences; acknowledge this without dwelling. Energy is typically highest in the PTW section (operationally relevant) and can dip during the legal register (procedural) — use the significance scoring formula as an energiser.

### 09:00–10:30 | Content Block 1 — Incident Recording & Investigation (90 min)

**Session aim**: Participants can record all HSE event types correctly and complete the investigation workflow

**Topics covered** (see `MODULE-HSE-WORKFLOWS.md`):
- Incident vs near miss vs observation — when to use each record type (15 min)
- Incident record fields: `title`, `dateOccurred`, `severity` (MINOR/MODERATE/MAJOR/CRITICAL/CATASTROPHIC), incident type, location, injured persons, immediate actions (20 min)
- Severity classification framework — case examples from MINOR to CATASTROPHIC (15 min)
- Investigation workflow: contributing factors, immediate causes, root causes, corrective actions, witness statements (25 min)
- TRIR and LTIR calculation — how Nexara computes these from incident records (10 min)
- Near miss management — why near miss frequency is a leading indicator (5 min)

**Transition**: "After the break, we'll move from incidents to the legal framework — the register where you manage your obligations and the environmental aspects module."

### 10:45–12:15 | Content Block 2 — Legal Register & Environmental Compliance (90 min)

**Topics covered** (see `MODULE-LEGAL-ENVIRONMENTAL.md`):
- Legal register structure: obligation types (Regulation, Directive, Permit, Standard, Contractual), jurisdiction, applicability criteria (25 min)
- Adding new obligations: mandatory fields, review date management, compliance status (COMPLIANT/AT_RISK/NON_COMPLIANT) (20 min)
- Evidence linking: attaching compliance evidence, what constitutes adequate evidence for each obligation type (20 min)
- Environmental aspects and impacts: the aspect/impact relationship, significance scoring formula, significant aspects list (25 min)

**Significance scoring formula** (key teaching point):
`Score = severity×1.5 + probability×1.5 + duration + extent + reversibility + regulatory + stakeholder`
Score ≥ 15 = Significant aspect (requires operational control or objective)

**Transition**: "After lunch, we'll look at the operational controls side — Permit to Work and inspections. These are where HSE management meets day-to-day operations."
