# Assessment — Day B: Health, Safety & Environment

**Format**: 20 MCQ | **Time**: 30 minutes | **Pass**: ≥ 75% (15/20) | **Delivery**: `/module-owner/hse/assessment`

---

**Q1.** A worker trips on a trailing cable, stumbles, and catches themselves on a railing without injury. No damage to equipment or property. This event should be recorded as:
- A) An Incident (MINOR)
- B) A Near Miss
- C) An Observation
- D) No record is required — no harm occurred

**Q2.** The `dateOccurred` field on an incident record should contain:
- A) The date the investigation was completed
- B) The date the incident was first reported to the HSE manager
- C) The date and time the incident actually occurred
- D) The date the regulatory notification was submitted

**Q3.** A contractor is hospitalised for 6 hours with a suspected spinal injury after falling from scaffolding. They return to modified duties after 4 weeks. Which severity level applies?
- A) MODERATE
- B) MAJOR
- C) CRITICAL
- D) CATASTROPHIC

**Q4.** Which of the following metrics is a **leading** indicator of HSE performance?
- A) Lost Time Incident Rate (LTIR)
- B) Total Recordable Incident Rate (TRIR)
- C) Near miss frequency rate
- D) Number of fatalities in the period

**Q5.** TRIR is calculated using which formula?
- A) (Number of recordable incidents × 1,000) ÷ Total hours worked
- B) (Number of recordable incidents × 200,000) ÷ Total hours worked
- C) (Number of LTIs × 200,000) ÷ Total employees × 12
- D) (Number of fatalities + LTIs) ÷ Total hours worked × 100,000

**Q6.** When must the `regulatoryReportable` flag be set on an incident record?
- A) All incidents must be flagged as regulatory reportable
- B) Only when the incident involves an external contractor
- C) When the incident meets the threshold for mandatory regulatory notification (e.g. RIDDOR)
- D) Only when a fatality has occurred

**Q7.** In the Nexara legal register, which compliance status should be used when a new regulation has been identified and an action plan to achieve compliance is actively under way?
- A) COMPLIANT
- B) AT_RISK
- C) NON_COMPLIANT
- D) PENDING_REVIEW

**Q8.** The review date on a legal register obligation triggers:
- A) Automatic revocation of the obligation if no action is taken
- B) An email reminder to the obligation owner to confirm whether the regulation has changed and update the status
- C) A formal internal audit of compliance with the obligation
- D) Escalation to SUPER_ADMIN for management review input

**Q9.** Under ISO 14001:2015, which of the following best describes an environmental "aspect"?
- A) The negative change in the environment (e.g. air pollution)
- B) An element of the organisation's activities that can interact with the environment
- C) A legal obligation relating to environmental performance
- D) A target for reducing the organisation's environmental footprint

**Q10.** Using the Nexara significance formula, an aspect scores: severity=4, probability=4, duration=3, extent=2, reversibility=3, regulatory=4, stakeholder=3. What is the significance score?
- A) 23
- B) 27
- C) 31
- D) 33

**Q11.** An environmental aspect with a significance score of 12 requires:
- A) An environmental objective and target
- B) Operational controls and emergency procedures
- C) Both A and B
- D) Neither — score < 15 means the aspect is not significant

**Q12.** Which work activity does NOT typically require a Permit to Work in the Nexara standard template library?
- A) Confined space entry
- B) Roof access (work at height)
- C) Routine office cleaning
- D) Hot work in a non-designated area

**Q13.** A PTW approver has been on annual leave for 3 days. A permit is awaiting their approval and cannot wait. The correct action is:
- A) Allow the permit holder to proceed — the approver can sign retrospectively
- B) Use Actions → Reassign Approval to delegate to a nominated substitute; log the reason
- C) Cancel the permit and re-raise it when the approver returns
- D) Contact Nexara Support to override the approval chain

**Q14.** A PTW has expired and the permit holder has not submitted a closure record. What does the Live Permits Dashboard show?
- A) The permit is removed from the dashboard automatically
- B) The permit is shown with a red alert as an expired permit awaiting closure
- C) The permit holder is locked out of the system until closure is submitted

**Q15.** During a monthly housekeeping inspection, an inspector finds fire extinguishers in Zone C have not been serviced for 18 months (requirement: annual). The correct finding classification is:
- A) Observation
- B) Minor Non-Conformance
- C) Major Non-Conformance
- D) Catastrophic — immediate evacuation required

**Q16.** Which ISO 45001:2018 clause covers the requirement to investigate incidents and near misses?
- A) Clause 6.1.2 — Hazard identification
- B) Clause 8.1.3 — Management of change
- C) Clause 10.2 — Incident, nonconformity and corrective action
- D) Clause 9.1.1 — Monitoring, measurement, analysis and evaluation

**Q17.** Compliance evidence for a regulatory obligation should be:
- A) The text of the regulation itself, downloaded from the government website
- B) The organisation's procedure implementing the regulation plus evidence of its application (e.g. training records, inspection records)
- C) A signed letter from the regulatory authority confirming compliance
- D) A copy of the ISO 45001 certificate, as certification demonstrates compliance with all regulations

**Q18.** A significant environmental aspect requires which of the following? (Select the most complete answer.)
- A) Only an environmental objective and target
- B) Only an operational control
- C) At minimum one of: an operational control, an environmental objective and target, or an emergency procedure (depending on the nature of the aspect)
- D) Both an objective AND an operational control AND an emergency procedure — all three are always required

**Q19.** An organisation's LTIR for the current year is 3.4. Last year it was 2.1. What is the most appropriate response?
- A) No action — LTIR fluctuates naturally and cannot be managed
- B) Conduct a trend analysis: review the types and causes of the LTIs that increased the rate; identify whether there is a common theme requiring systemic corrective action
- C) Immediately suspend all high-risk operations until the LTIR returns to 2.1
- D) Reduce the denominator by recording fewer hours worked in the system

**Q20.** An ISO 45001 surveillance auditor requests evidence that the organisation is systematically identifying hazards and assessing risks. Which Nexara output is most appropriate?
- A) TRIR and LTIR dashboard export
- B) The list of all open incidents in the period
- C) The environmental aspects register with significance scores
- D) The risk assessment register or hazard identification records from the H&S module, mapped to ISO 45001 clause 6.1.2
