# Module Owner Training — Learning Objectives

Objectives are written to Bloom's Taxonomy levels: **Remember (R)**, **Understand (U)**, **Apply (A)**, **Analyse (An)**, **Evaluate (Ev)**, **Create (C)**.

---

## Day A — Quality & Non-Conformance

By the end of Day A, participants will be able to:

1. **(A)** Create, categorise, and assign a non-conformance (NC) record in the Quality module, selecting the correct severity, category (Product, Process, System, Supplier), and responsible owner
2. **(A)** Link a CAPA action to a non-conformance and track the CAPA through investigation, action planning, implementation, and effectiveness review stages
3. **(An)** Interpret the quality KPI dashboard — including NC open rate, closure within SLA, and repeat NC rate — and identify which metrics require corrective action
4. **(A)** Configure and generate a quality management report for a specified date range, NC category, and organisational unit, and export it in PDF and CSV format
5. **(Ev)** Apply ISO 9001:2015 clause mapping to categorise non-conformances against relevant standard requirements and generate an ISO evidence package for internal audit preparation

---

## Day B — Health, Safety & Environment

By the end of Day B, participants will be able to:

1. **(A)** Record an incident or near miss in the HSE module with correct severity classification (using the MINOR/MODERATE/MAJOR/CRITICAL/CATASTROPHIC taxonomy), evidence upload, and responsible party assignment
2. **(A)** Manage entries on the legal register: add new obligations, link compliance evidence, set review due dates, and close obligations with documented evidence of compliance
3. **(A)** Create and manage an environmental aspect/impact record, apply the significance scoring formula, and identify which aspects require operational controls or objectives
4. **(A)** Create a Permit to Work (PTW) request, configure approver chains, manage live permits on the dashboard, and formally close a permit with required evidence
5. **(An)** Analyse HSE KPIs — including TRIR (Total Recordable Incident Rate), LTIR (Lost Time Incident Rate), and near miss frequency — and produce ISO 45001/14001 evidence packages for management review

---

## Day C — HR & Payroll

By the end of Day C, participants will be able to:

1. **(A)** Manage the full employee record lifecycle in the HR module — from onboarding (starter checklist, contract upload, role assignment) through to leavers (exit checklist, access termination flag, data retention)
2. **(A)** Assign training courses to individuals and groups, track completion against compliance deadlines, and generate a training compliance matrix report identifying overdue assignments
3. **(A)** Configure and run a payroll period in the Payroll module — including pay adjustments, reviewing the payroll audit trail, and confirming the journal export for finance system integration
4. **(An)** Interpret HR analytics: headcount trend, absence rate, training completion percentage, and payroll variance — and identify records requiring follow-up action
5. **(Ev)** Apply GDPR data-handling obligations to HR records: understand retention periods, right-to-erasure processes, and generate a data processing summary for a nominated employee

---

## Day D — Finance & Contracts

By the end of Day D, participants will be able to:

1. **(A)** Create and manage financial records (income, expenditure, and budget entries) in the Finance module, apply the PO/invoice approval workflow, and reconcile a variance between budget and actual
2. **(A)** Create a contract record with all lifecycle stages — draft, review, execution, active, milestone tracking, renewal alert, and termination — including version-controlled document uploads
3. **(A)** Evaluate a supplier using the scorecard framework: input performance scores against weighted criteria, calculate the overall rating, and change the supplier's qualification status (Preferred / Approved / Conditional / Disqualified)
4. **(An)** Analyse the finance KPI dashboard — budget utilisation, accounts payable ageing, and contract expiry pipeline — and generate a management-ready financial summary report
5. **(Ev)** Identify supply chain risk indicators on the supplier register, apply risk-level classification, and generate a preferred supplier list report for procurement decision-making

---

## Day E — Audits, CAPA & Management Review

By the end of Day E, participants will be able to:

1. **(A)** Build an annual audit programme in the Audit module — including scheduling, scope definition, assignment of lead auditors and auditees, and distribution of the audit plan
2. **(A)** Conduct an audit workflow end-to-end: opening meeting set-up, sampling and evidence recording, finding classification (conformance, observation, minor NC, major NC, opportunity for improvement), report generation, and distribution
3. **(A)** Facilitate a root cause analysis using 5-Why, fishbone (Ishikawa), or fault tree techniques; record the analysis in the CAPA module; and set up an action plan with owners, due dates, and effectiveness review criteria
4. **(A)** Compile management review inputs from all active IMS modules — including quality KPIs, HSE metrics, HR data, finance summary, and audit findings — using the Management Review module's input compiler, and record outputs as tracked actions
5. **(C)** Generate a complete ISO management system evidence package — linking audit reports, CAPA actions, legal compliance evidence, and management review outputs — suitable for submission to a certification body during an external audit
