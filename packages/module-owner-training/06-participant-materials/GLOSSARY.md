# Module Owner Training — Glossary

60+ terms covering quality, HSE, HR/payroll, finance/contracts, audits, CAPA, and management review.

---

## A

**Aspect (Environmental)**: An element of an organisation's activities, products, or services that can interact with the environment. Contrast with Impact (the environmental change that results).

**Audit Finding**: A conclusion from an audit based on objective evidence. Types: Conformance, Observation, OFI, Minor NC, Major NC.

**Audit Programme**: The planned set of audits for a defined period, based on risk and importance of the processes to be audited (ISO 9001 cl. 9.2).

**Audit Trail**: A chronological, append-only record of all actions taken on a record in Nexara. Cannot be modified or deleted. Primary ISO evidence source.

---

## B

**BIK (Benefit-in-Kind)**: A non-cash benefit provided to an employee that is taxable. Must be recorded in Nexara Payroll for P11D reporting.

**Budget Utilisation**: The percentage of the approved budget that has been spent to date. Formula: Actual / Budget × 100.

---

## C

**CAPA (Corrective and Preventive Action)**: A structured process for identifying the root cause of a non-conformance or other problem and implementing actions to prevent recurrence (corrective) or prevent occurrence (preventive).

**Certification Body**: An accredited third party that audits management systems against ISO standards and issues certification. Examples: BSI, Bureau Veritas, SGS, Intertek.

**Clause Mapping**: The process of linking records in Nexara to the specific clauses of an ISO standard they evidence compliance with.

**Competence (ISO 9001 cl. 7.2)**: The ability to apply knowledge and skills to achieve intended results. Demonstrated in Nexara via training records and competency assessments.

**Conformance**: An audit finding indicating that the audited area meets the requirements of the standard or procedure. Not recorded as a finding in Nexara (absence of a finding = conformance).

**Containment Action**: Immediate steps taken to control and isolate a non-conformance to prevent further impact. Distinct from corrective action.

**Contract Lifecycle**: The stages a contract passes through: Draft → Under Review → Pending Signature → Active → Expiring → Expired → Terminated.

**Corrective Action**: An action to eliminate the root cause of a detected non-conformance or undesirable situation (ISO 9001 cl. 10.2). Prevents recurrence.

**Cost Centre**: An organisational unit used to allocate and track expenditure. Used in Nexara Finance for budget vs actual reporting.

---

## D

**DENY Override**: An explicit access denial in Nexara RBAC that overrides any role-granted permission for a specific namespace.

**Document Control**: The systematic management of documented information including creation, review, approval, versioning, distribution, and archiving (ISO 9001 cl. 7.5).

---

## E

**Effectiveness Review**: A scheduled evaluation of whether a CAPA corrective action has achieved its intended outcome — i.e., the non-conformance has not recurred.

**Environmental Objective**: A measurable target set by an organisation in relation to its significant environmental aspects (ISO 14001 cl. 6.2).

**EHS**: Environment, Health and Safety — often used interchangeably with HSE.

---

## F

**Fault Tree Analysis**: A root cause analysis technique using top-down Boolean logic (AND/OR gates) to identify combinations of causes that could lead to a top-level failure event.

**Fishbone Diagram (Ishikawa)**: A root cause analysis technique that categorises potential causes of a problem into six branches: Man, Machine, Method, Material, Measurement, Environment.

**5-Why**: A root cause analysis technique that asks "Why?" five times to drill down from a symptom to the underlying root cause.

**Finding (Audit)**: A conclusion documented during an audit. See: Observation, OFI, Minor NC, Major NC.

**Framework Agreement**: A contract that establishes the terms under which individual orders/call-offs can be made over a period of time.

---

## G

**GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)**: EU and UK privacy regulation governing the processing of personal data. Applies to all employee records in Nexara HR.

---

## H

**HSE (Health, Safety and Environment)**: Collective term for health, safety, and environmental management. Also the UK Health and Safety Executive (regulator).

---

## I

**Impact (Environmental)**: The change in the environment resulting from an environmental aspect — positive or negative.

**Incident**: An unplanned event that resulted in injury, illness, property damage, or environmental release.

**IR35**: UK tax legislation determining whether a contractor is effectively an employee for tax purposes. Flag in Nexara HR contractor records.

**ISO 9001:2015**: International standard for quality management systems.

**ISO 14001:2015**: International standard for environmental management systems.

**ISO 45001:2018**: International standard for occupational health and safety management systems.

---

## J

**Journal Export**: The payroll file exported from Nexara Payroll for import into the finance system's chart of accounts. Contains period totals by account code and cost centre.

---

## L

**Leaver Process**: The structured set of actions completed when an employee leaves an organisation. Includes exit checklist, termination date, access revocation, and data retention flag.

**Legal Register**: A documented inventory of all legal, regulatory, and contractual obligations applicable to the organisation's HSE activities.

**Lost Time Injury (LTI)**: A work-related injury or illness that results in the affected person being unable to return to work on their next scheduled shift.

**LTIR (Lost Time Incident Rate)**: The rate of lost time injuries per 200,000 hours worked. Formula: (LTIs × 200,000) ÷ Total hours worked.

---

## M

**Major NC**: An audit finding indicating a systemic failure or a situation that could directly threaten certification.

**Management Review**: A formal, periodic review by top management of the organisation's management system to ensure its continuing suitability, adequacy, effectiveness, and alignment with strategic direction (ISO 9001 cl. 9.3).

**Milestone (Contract)**: A key date or event in a contract lifecycle with an associated alert. Examples: review dates, payment milestones, renewal decision points.

**Minor NC**: An audit finding indicating a non-compliance with a procedure or requirement that does not create immediate risk or threaten certification.

---

## N

**NC (Non-Conformance)**: A failure to meet a requirement (ISO 9001 cl. 8.7, 10.2). Can be a product, process, system, or supplier non-conformance.

**Near Miss**: An unplanned event that had the potential to cause harm but did not result in injury, illness, or damage.

---

## O

**Observation (HSE)**: A condition or behaviour that, if uncorrected, could lead to an incident or near miss.

**Observation (Audit)**: An audit finding noting a potential area for improvement that does not yet constitute a non-conformance.

**OFI (Opportunity for Improvement)**: An audit finding suggesting a positive recommendation for improvement, without any non-conformance.

**Obligation (Legal Register)**: A specific legal, regulatory, or contractual requirement applicable to the organisation.

**Operational Control**: A defined procedure, process, or technical control that manages a significant environmental aspect or HSE risk.

---

## P

**Payroll Period**: A defined period for which payroll is calculated and processed (weekly, fortnightly, monthly, or custom).

**Pay Adjustment**: A modification to a standard payroll calculation — overtime, bonus, deduction, benefit-in-kind, or statutory pay.

**Preventive Action**: An action to eliminate the cause of a potential non-conformance or other undesirable situation before it occurs.

**Preferred Supplier**: A supplier who has achieved the highest qualification status through evaluation, demonstrating consistent quality, delivery, and compliance performance.

**PTW (Permit to Work)**: A formal, documented authorisation system for high-risk work activities, requiring completion of pre-work checks and approval before work commences.

---

## Q

**Qualification Status (Supplier)**: The classification of a supplier's standing: PREFERRED, APPROVED, CONDITIONAL, DISQUALIFIED, or UNDER_REVIEW.

---

## R

**Regulatory Reportable**: An incident that meets the threshold for mandatory notification to a regulatory authority (e.g. RIDDOR in the UK, OSHA in the USA).

**Repeat NC**: A non-conformance that recurs with the same root cause category, NC category, and process area within a 12-month window.

**Repeat Findings Rate**: The percentage of audit findings that are repeats of previous findings. Indicates CAPA system effectiveness.

**RIDDOR**: Reporting of Injuries, Diseases and Dangerous Occurrences Regulations 2013 (UK). Requires reporting of specified workplace injuries and near-miss dangerous occurrences to the HSE.

**Root Cause**: The fundamental, underlying cause of a problem — the cause that, if addressed, would prevent recurrence.

**RPO (Recovery Point Objective)**: The maximum acceptable data loss in the event of a system failure, measured in time.

**RTO (Recovery Time Objective)**: The maximum acceptable time to restore a system after a failure.

---

## S

**Scorecard (Supplier)**: A structured evaluation tool that scores a supplier against weighted criteria to determine their qualification status.

**Significance Scoring**: The Nexara formula used to determine whether an environmental aspect is significant: Score = severity×1.5 + probability×1.5 + duration + extent + reversibility + regulatory + stakeholder. Score ≥ 15 = Significant.

**SLA (Service Level Agreement)**: A commitment to deliver a service within defined performance parameters. Example: NC closure within 30 days.

**SMART**: Criteria for effective action planning — Specific, Measurable, Assignable/Achievable, Realistic, Time-bound.

---

## T

**TRIR (Total Recordable Incident Rate)**: The rate of recordable incidents per 200,000 hours worked. Formula: (Recordable incidents × 200,000) ÷ Total hours worked.

**Toolbox Talk**: A short, informal safety briefing conducted at the workplace before a work activity begins. Should be documented in Nexara H&S.

**Training Compliance Matrix**: A grid showing completion status for each mandatory course by employee and department.

---

## V

**Validity Period (Training)**: The duration for which a training certificate is considered current before a refresher is required.

**Variance (Payroll)**: The difference between the current period's gross payroll and the previous period's. Variances >5% are flagged; >15% require investigation before journal export.

**Version Control**: The systematic management of document revisions to ensure only the current version is in use and previous versions are archived.

---

## W

**Weighted Score (Supplier)**: The supplier evaluation score calculated by multiplying each criterion score by its weighting percentage and summing the results.
