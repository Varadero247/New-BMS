## Question Bank

**Q1.** In Nexara, an annual audit programme record is created as a parent record from which individual audits are created as children. What is the primary advantage of this structure over creating individual audit records independently?
- A) Child audit records inherit the programme lead's system permissions automatically
- B) The programme provides a managed view of all planned audits, supports risk-based scheduling, and enables programme-level reporting on findings rates and completion status
- C) Individual audit records cannot be created in Nexara without a parent programme record
- D) The programme structure reduces the number of records the system creates, improving performance

**Q2.** An internal auditor finds that a single training record for one employee out of 47 reviewed is missing the trainer's signature. What is the most appropriate finding classification?
- A) Major NC — documented information is missing
- B) Minor NC — a single, isolated instance of non-fulfilment with no systemic impact
- C) OFI — the training records are generally good; this is an improvement opportunity
- D) Observation — the records are largely compliant; this is a risk indicator

**Q3.** An internal audit of ISO 9001 clause 7.5 finds that the organisation has a thorough and well-maintained document control system. The auditor identifies one department that has adopted a particularly effective practice of indexing its controlled documents using QR codes linked to the master document in the system. What finding classification is most appropriate?
- A) Conformance — this is a standard finding that the requirement is met
- B) Observation — QR code indexing creates a risk of documents being accessed without version control
- C) OFI — this is a positive practice that could be beneficially applied in other departments
- D) Minor NC — QR code indexing is not specified in the document control procedure and therefore constitutes a deviation

**Q4.** A Major NC is raised when which condition is present?
- A) A single instance of a minor documentation error is found during an internal audit
- B) A systemic failure to meet a standard requirement, or a failure posing significant risk to product safety, customer satisfaction, or management system integrity
- C) Any finding against a clause 8 requirement of ISO 9001:2015
- D) The auditee is unable to provide records requested by the auditor during the opening meeting

**Q5.** Under ISO 9001:2015 clause 9.2.2, the organisation must retain documented information as evidence of the audit programme and audit results. In Nexara, which combination of records provides this evidence?
- A) The audit schedule only — individual audit records are not required
- B) The annual programme record, individual audit records (including scope, dates, auditors, auditees), finding records with ISO clause references, and the distributed audit report
- C) The audit report only — the individual finding records are supporting documents, not evidence
- D) The management review minutes, which summarise the audit programme results

**Q6.** The 5-Why technique in Nexara requires all five levels to be completed before "root cause confirmed" status is permitted. What is the primary reason for this system-enforced requirement?
- A) ISO 9001 clause 10.2.2 specifically mandates a minimum of five root cause levels for all CAPAs
- B) Five levels is an arbitrary standard that Nexara uses for all RCA methods
- C) Stopping before five levels risks identifying a symptom rather than the root cause, leading to corrective actions that produce temporary improvement followed by recurrence
- D) The first four levels are required for evidence purposes; the fifth level triggers the action planning workflow

**Q7.** An HSE incident investigation reveals the following contributing causes: the technician was fatigued (overnight shift), the equipment guard had been temporarily removed for maintenance and not refitted, the work permit did not specify PPE requirements for this activity, the correct PPE was not available in the store, and the pre-task inspection form did not include a PPE check. Which root cause analysis technique is most appropriate for this scenario?
- A) 5-Why — the linear chain from PPE not worn to injury is straightforward
- B) Fault tree — the exact sequence of AND/OR gate logic must be modelled for safety-critical analysis
- C) Fishbone (Ishikawa) — multiple causes across Man, Machine, Method, Material, and Measurement categories are present simultaneously
- D) Any technique is equally appropriate; the choice has no impact on the corrective actions generated

**Q8.** A CAPA effectiveness review is marked "Ineffective." What does this status indicate and what is the required next step in Nexara?
- A) The CAPA actions were partially effective; the QMS Manager should extend the effectiveness review date and monitor for another 30 days
- B) The NC or finding has recurred; the original CAPA is closed as Ineffective and a new CAPA must be raised with a deeper or different root cause analysis method
- C) The effectiveness review was conducted too early; return the CAPA to In Progress and reschedule the review
- D) The CAPA actions were all marked complete but the evidence was insufficient; upload additional evidence and re-submit for review

**Q9.** A QMS Manager reviews the Repeat Findings Rate KPI and finds it is 26% for the current 12-month period. Three of the repeat findings are against the same ISO clause (8.4 — control of externally provided processes). What escalation action does this require?

- B) Raise a new CAPA for each repeat finding; no escalation to senior management is required until the rate exceeds 50%
- C) Escalate to senior management as a management review input; three or more repeat findings against the same clause indicate a systemic CAPA failure requiring strategic intervention
