# Day E — Audits, CAPA & Management Review: Full Day Schedule

**Programme**: Nexara Certified Module Owner — Audits, CAPA & Management Review
**Duration**: 08:30–17:00 | 7 CPD Hours
**Audience**: Audit leads, QMS managers, management review secretaries, internal audit professionals
**Maximum Group Size**: 12 participants
**CPD Recognition**: IIA (Institute of Internal Auditors) accepted

---

## Facilitator Energy Note

Day E participants are your most experienced group. They arrive with existing audit methodology knowledge, strong opinions about what good audit practice looks like, and — frequently — frustration about the gap between their professional standards and the reality of how auditing is actually conducted in their organisations. Lean into this expertise rather than trying to teach fundamentals they already know.

The opening round-table should be used to surface this experience: "Tell me how your current audit programme compares to what you'd consider best practice" is a far more productive ice-breaker for this cohort than a standard introduction. You will hear themes — disconnected systems, findings that never make it into CAPA, management reviews that are box-ticking exercises — that you can then address directly in each content block.

In the audit section, do not dwell on what constitutes a non-conformance. This group knows. Focus instead on what Nexara does that their current system does not: the direct link from audit finding to CAPA, the clause-mapping in reports, and the annual programme view rather than standalone audits.

For the CAPA section, the root cause analysis techniques are where the real learning happens. Many experienced practitioners have learned 5-Why in isolation; the comparison between 5-Why, fishbone, and fault tree — and the worked examples of when to use each — consistently generates the most discussion of the day.

Anchor the management review section firmly in the real-world outcome: "This is the pack that goes to the CEO, and this is what the certification body examiner will ask to see." The input compiler demonstration — where live data from all modules flows into the review — is typically the moment this group stops comparing Nexara to what they have and starts thinking about what they want.

## Full Timetable

### 08:30–09:00 | Welcome & Orientation (30 min)

**Session aim**: Establish group expertise profile, set day objectives, anchor to real-world audit and management system experience

**Activities**:
- Facilitator introduces themselves and Nexara training team (3 min)
- Round-table introductions: name, organisation, role, and "how does your current audit programme compare to best practice — honestly?" (15 min)
- Day E objectives and schedule walkthrough — emphasise the lab scenario (Vertex Global Systems ISO 9001 surveillance preparation) and the advanced nature of the assessment (5 min)
- Sandbox environment login check — every participant must confirm access to the Vertex Global Systems environment before 09:00 ends (5 min)
- Parking lot introduced — this group will generate significant questions; maintain a visible parking lot throughout the day (2 min)

**Facilitator note**: Vertex Global Systems sandbox contains a pre-built quality management system with existing NC records, a partially completed CAPA, and a management review record in Planned status. Walk participants through the sandbox structure briefly at orientation — unlike previous days, participants will be building on existing data rather than starting from scratch. Ensure all participants can navigate to Audits, Quality, and Management Review modules.

---

### 09:00–10:30 | Content Block 1 — Audit Management (90 min)

**Session aim**: Participants understand the full audit lifecycle in Nexara — from annual programme planning to finding classification, report generation, and distribution

**Topics covered** (see `MODULE-AUDIT-MANAGEMENT.md` for full content):

- **Annual audit programme** (20 min): The annual programme as a parent record containing all planned audits — risk-based scheduling, programme scope, how child audit records are created from the programme, and the common mistake of managing audits as standalone records. What an annual programme looks like on the Nexara dashboard.
- **Scheduling individual audits** (15 min): Audit type selection (Internal/External/Surveillance/Certification), scope definition (clauses, processes, departments), lead auditor assignment, conflict checking, and how the audit schedule view looks for the audit lead and auditees
- **Audit conduct** (20 min): Opening meeting record, sampling plan, evidence collection by linking records from other modules, interview notes field, and what constitutes sufficient audit evidence under ISO 19011
- **Finding classification** (20 min): The five classifications in detail — Conformance, Observation, OFI, Minor NC, Major NC — with worked examples for each. The difference between an Observation and an OFI is a frequent source of confusion; address it with a decision tree exercise. Linking findings to specific ISO clauses.
- **Audit report and distribution** (15 min): Auto-generated report contents, distribution list configuration, email distribution with read-receipt tracking, and how the report becomes the evidence for the audit in the certification body's eyes

**Transition cue**: "Every finding in an audit is only as valuable as the corrective action it generates. After the break, we'll look at the CAPA process in depth — and in particular, how you ensure your root cause analysis actually identifies the root cause rather than the most convenient answer."

### 10:45–12:15 | Content Block 2 — CAPA Root Cause Analysis (90 min)

**Session aim**: Participants can select and apply the appropriate root cause analysis technique in Nexara, plan effective corrective actions, conduct effectiveness reviews, and interpret the repeat findings rate KPI

**Topics covered** (see `MODULE-CAPA-WORKFLOWS.md` for full content):

- **Multi-source CAPA management** (15 min): How CAPAs originate from multiple source types in Nexara (NC records, audit findings, management review outputs, regulatory notices, customer complaints) and how to view all open CAPAs across all sources in one view
- **5-Why technique** (20 min): Structured five-level questioning in Nexara — each Why as a separate field, the system validation that requires all 5 levels before confirming root cause, worked example with a quality NC, common mistake of stopping at the symptom rather than the root cause
- **Fishbone (Ishikawa) technique** (20 min): The six categories (Man, Machine, Method, Material, Measurement, Environment), entering causes into each branch, the visual fishbone diagram the system generates, worked example with an HSE incident, when to use fishbone vs 5-Why
- **Fault tree technique** (15 min): Top-level event, gate logic (AND/OR), contributing events, fault tree diagram generation, when fault tree is appropriate (complex multi-causal events, safety-critical failures), comparison with the other two techniques
- **Action planning and effectiveness review** (10 min): SMART criteria enforcement, effectiveness review outcomes (Effective, Partially Effective, Ineffective), what each outcome requires as a next step
- **Repeat findings rate** (10 min): KPI definition, what it indicates, escalation triggers, the organisational culture issues it surfaces

**Transition cue**: "After lunch, we'll look at how all of this — the audits, the CAPAs, the performance data from every module — flows into the management review. This is where the management system tells its story to top management."

### 13:00–14:15 | Content Block 3 — Management Review (75 min)

**Session aim**: Participants can create a management review record, use the input compiler to aggregate data from all modules, structure agenda and minutes, create and track output actions, and generate the ISO evidence package for a certification body

**Topics covered** (see `MODULE-MGMT-REVIEW.md` for full content):

- **Management review architecture** (15 min): ISO 9001/14001/45001 clause 9.3 requirements, the Nexara management review record structure (chairperson, attendees, status progression), what "planned intervals" means in practice, and how Nexara enforces the review cycle
- **Input compiler** (25 min): Live demonstration of the input compiler — compiling data from Quality, HSE, HR, Finance, Audits, and Risk modules in real time. What each compiled input shows, how inputs are timestamped and source-linked, and why this is the most significant efficiency gain Day E participants typically identify. The scenario: "How long does it currently take you to compile the inputs for a management review? And how long would it take in Nexara?"
- **Agenda, minutes and output actions** (20 min): Structured agenda items with time allocation and presenter, outcome recording per agenda item, the distinction between management review output actions (strategic) and CAPA actions (operational), tracking output actions on the management review dashboard
- **ISO evidence package for certification body** (15 min): What the ISO evidence package for management review contains, how it maps to ISO 9001 clause 9.3, 14001 clause 9.3, and 45001 clause 9.3, and the examination of this package during surveillance and recertification audits
