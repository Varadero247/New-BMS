# On-Site Delivery Guide — Module Owner Training

---

## Room Setup

### Preferred Layout: Horseshoe / U-Shape

The horseshoe layout is optimal for module owner training because:
- All participants can see the facilitator screen and each other
- The facilitator can circulate easily during the lab
- Side conversations (which are productive for this audience) are natural

**Room dimensions**: Minimum 6m × 8m for 14 participants in horseshoe layout

**Avoid**: Classroom rows — too formal and discourages peer learning. Theatre style — prevents lab work.

## Lab Exercise Management (On-Site)

On-site lab management differs from virtual in one key way: you can see what participants are doing in real time.

**Circulation strategy**:
- Walk the horseshoe slowly during independent work steps
- Pause briefly behind each participant — observe without interrupting
- Note participants who are struggling (empty sandbox, confused expression)
- Approach struggling participants quietly: "How are you getting on with Step [n]? What have you tried?"
- Do not provide the answer directly — ask questions: "What does the [field name] field tell you? What would you expect to see there?"

**Sticky-note system** (optional but effective):
- Give each participant two sticky notes: green and red
- Green = "I'm fine"; Red = "I need help"
- Participants post their green/red on their workstation during independent work
- Facilitator prioritises red-stickied stations

## Materials Security

**Answer keys**: Never leave answer keys unattended on the facilitator's desk during breaks. Lock them in a bag or briefcase. The answer key is the only document that should never be visible to participants.

**Assessment papers (backup)**: If using printed assessment papers, collect all copies immediately after submission. Do not allow participants to photograph or retain assessment questions.

**Certificates**: Pre-signed blank certificates should be kept in a sealed envelope until the ceremony. Do not leave them on the facilitator desk where they could be viewed.

---

## Late Arrivals

If a participant arrives after 09:00:
- Seat them with minimal disruption
- Slide their materials to them quietly
- During the first break, give them a 5-minute catch-up briefing on what they missed
- If they arrive after 10:30, they have missed >20% of content: discuss privately whether to continue or attend the next cohort

A participant who has missed more than 25% of the day's content should not sit the assessment and should be registered for the next cohort. Issue them a partial attendance record only.
