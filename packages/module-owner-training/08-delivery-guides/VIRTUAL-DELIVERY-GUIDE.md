# Virtual Delivery Guide — Module Owner Training

---

## Platform Setup (Zoom or Microsoft Teams)

### Pre-Session Configuration

**Zoom**:
- Enable Breakout Rooms (for lab exercises): Settings → In Meeting → Breakout Rooms
- Enable Polling (for knowledge checks): Settings → In Meeting → Polling
- Enable Whiteboard (for facilitation diagrams): Settings → In Meeting → Whiteboard
- Pre-create polls for each content block (see polling questions below)
- Set waiting room: on (to manage late arrivals)
- Recording: enable cloud recording (for participants who request a replay)

**Microsoft Teams**:
- Create a dedicated Team for the cohort (do not use ad-hoc meetings — you need the persistent chat)
- Enable polls via Microsoft Forms integration
- Breakout rooms: available in Teams — configure before session
- Enable Transcript: ensures participants can review spoken content

### Technical Requirements for Participants

- Reliable internet connection (minimum 5 Mbps download)
- Chrome or Edge browser (latest version) for the Nexara sandbox
- Headset with microphone recommended
- Second screen recommended (one for Zoom/Teams; one for the sandbox)
- Sandbox login credentials: issued 24 hours before the session

## Lab Exercise Adaptation for Virtual Delivery

### Breakout Room Structure

Each lab exercise uses breakout rooms of 3–4 participants:
- Assign participants to breakout rooms before the session begins
- Each breakout room has a named discussion lead (rotate per session)
- Lab steps are completed individually but discussed within the breakout room
- Facilitator rotates between breakout rooms during the 75-minute lab
- Last 10 minutes: return to main room for group debrief

### Screen Share During Lab

Facilitator screen share setup:
- Share the Nexara sandbox — ensure only the relevant module is visible
- Use "Share portion of screen" to avoid showing facilitator notes or the answer key
- Ask participants to share their screen when demonstrating a step (rotate through 2–3 participants per step)

### Guidance for Participants Who Fall Behind

Virtual labs move faster than on-site labs because participants cannot easily observe each other. Signs a participant is falling behind:
- Silent in breakout room (check via messaging)
- Not responding to step completion check-ins

Recovery: Send the participant a direct message with the expected sandbox state for the next step. Do not delay the group.

## Polling Questions (per Content Block)

### Day A — Quality
**Block 1**: "In your current process, how do you track NC status?" (options: Spreadsheet / Email / No tracking / Another system)
**Block 2**: "How many open NCs does your organisation currently have?" (options: 0-5 / 6-20 / 21-50 / 50+)

### Day B — HSE
**Block 1**: "Has your organisation had a RIDDOR/regulatory-reportable incident in the last 12 months?" (Yes / No / Unsure)
**Block 2**: "Do you have a legal register?" (Yes / No / Partial)

### Day C — HR & Payroll
**Block 1**: "Do you currently track training compliance in a spreadsheet?" (Yes / No — we use a system)
**Block 2**: "How many payroll periods do you run per month?" (1 / 2 / 4+ / We outsource)

### Day D — Finance & Contracts
**Block 1**: "Do you have a contract expiry alert system in place?" (Yes / No / Ad-hoc)
**Block 2**: "How do you currently evaluate suppliers?" (Formal scorecard / Informal review / No formal process)

### Day E — Advanced
**Block 1**: "When did you last conduct a management review?" (<3 months / 3-6 months / 6-12 months / >12 months)
**Block 2**: "What root cause analysis technique do you use most?" (5-Why / Fishbone / Fault Tree / None formal)

## Energy Management (Virtual)

Virtual sessions lose energy faster than on-site. Key interventions:

**90-minute block refresh techniques**:
- Poll at 30 minutes into each block
- Chat round ("Type your answer in chat: what's the most surprising thing we've covered so far?")
- Think-pair-share: "Spend 90 seconds thinking about how this applies to your role, then tell your breakout room"
- Screen share rotation: invite different participants to share their sandbox progress

**After lunch (13:00 restart)**:
- Start with a 5-question recap quiz (unscored) rather than diving straight into content
- Slightly increase interactivity in Content Block 3

**Lab timing**:
- Give a 5-minute warning before breakout rooms close
- Celebrate completed steps publicly: "I can see several of you have completed Step 4 — well done"

## Chat Rules

Establish at the start:
- Use chat for questions, observations, and reactions — the facilitator or a co-facilitator monitors chat
- Use "QUESTION:" prefix in chat for questions that need to be addressed (easier to scan)
- Mute when not speaking (remind participants)
- Turn cameras on when possible during discussion — off is acceptable for long content blocks

## Post-Session Digital Pack

After the session, send to all participants:
- PDF of the relevant participant handbook section
- Quick-reference card (PDF — laminate-ready)
- Their certificate (if passed) as PDF
- CPD slip (PDF)
- Sandbox access confirmation (14-day extension)
- Link to the Nexara knowledge base article for their programme module(s)
