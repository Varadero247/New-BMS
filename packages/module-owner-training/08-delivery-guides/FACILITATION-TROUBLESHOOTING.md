# Facilitation Troubleshooting — Module Owner Training

*Quick reference for common issues during delivery.*

---

## Technology Issues

### Issue: Sandbox not loading for all participants

**Immediate response**:
1. Ask participants to hard refresh (Ctrl+Shift+R / Cmd+Shift+R)
2. Check if the issue is the sandbox itself or the browser: try a different browser
3. If sandbox is down for all participants: switch to demonstration-only mode — facilitator demonstrates each lab step; participants follow along without independent sandbox access
4. For virtual delivery: switch to screen share where participants verbally direct the facilitator

**Pre-session prevention**: Always test sandbox access from a participant laptop/credential 30 minutes before the session starts.

### Issue: Portal assessment not loading at 16:30

**Immediate response**:
1. Distribute printed backup assessment papers (always have 5 copies prepared)
2. Participants complete on paper
3. Facilitator marks using the answer key immediately after collection
4. Issue results verbally and present certificates as normal
5. Submit paper results to Nexara Training within 24 hours for digital certificate generation

### Issue: Individual participant sandbox login failure

**Immediate response**:
1. During the session start check (08:30-09:00), identify this immediately
2. Ask the participant to try password reset if it's a credentials issue
3. If sandbox access cannot be resolved within 5 minutes: pair the participant with a neighbour for Content Blocks 1-2
4. During the break, contact Nexara IT Support: training-support@nexara.io
5. If access cannot be resolved before the lab: have the participant share a workstation for the lab exercise

## Pacing Issues

### Issue: Content block running 15+ minutes over time

**Diagnosis**: Too many Q&A diversions, or one section took longer than planned.

**Recovery options** (in order of preference):
1. **Cut from the KPI dashboard section** (15:45-16:30): This section can be replaced with a handout: "I'm going to send you a PDF walkthrough of the dashboard configuration — please review it this week."
2. **Compress Content Block 3**: If a deep-dive section is taking too long, cover the key points and refer participants to the module content file for the rest.
3. **Never cut the lab**: The lab is the most important learning activity and the best preparation for the assessment.
4. **Never cut the assessment**: The assessment is a programme requirement.

### Issue: Group moving through lab much faster than expected

**Response**:
- Direct early finishers to the Extension Task (included in every lab file)
- Add a supplementary discussion question: "Now that you've completed the workflow, what's one process in your organisation that this would change?"
- Prepare a second extension: "If you've done the extension task, try configuring a second dashboard widget or scheduling a second report."

## Participant Issues

### Issue: Participant significantly disrupting the group (persistent off-topic discussion)

**Response**:
1. Acknowledge their point: "That's a great real-world scenario — can we park that in the parking lot and come back to it at the debrief?"
2. Use break time for a private conversation: "I want to make sure we get through today's content — could we capture your remaining questions in the parking lot?"
3. Address parking lot questions at the debrief or by email after the session.

### Issue: Participant emotional distress during HSE incident recording (Day B)

**Specific to Day B**: The incident recording exercise can unexpectedly trigger emotions for participants who have experienced serious workplace incidents.

**Response**:
1. Do not draw attention to the participant in front of the group
2. During a natural break: check in privately — "Are you okay to continue? There's no obligation to share personal experiences."
3. The scenario uses fictional people and organisations — remind the participant of this if it helps
4. If the participant needs to step out, allow them to do so without explaining to the group
5. Offer a private debrief at the end of the day

### Issue: Participant challenges an assessment question (disagrees with the answer)

**Response**:
1. Thank them for raising it
2. Review the question and answer key reasoning together
3. If the question is genuinely ambiguous: note it for the Nexara Training team and inform the participant you'll escalate it
4. Never change an assessment score unilaterally — escalate to Nexara Training (training@nexara.io) for review
5. If the question is subsequently found to be in error, Nexara Training will reissue a corrected result

## Client / Organisational Issues

### Issue: Client requests to skip certain content ("we already know this")

**Response**: "I appreciate your team's expertise. Today's goal isn't to teach [topic] — it's to show you how Nexara implements [topic] so that your existing knowledge translates directly into the system. Let's move through the sections you know quickly and use the extra time for the lab."

Do not skip the content entirely — the assessment covers it.

### Issue: Client requests that participants use real production data in the sandbox

**Response**: "For data protection and assessment validity reasons, we always use the Nexara training sandbox with fictional data. Using real production data would mean: (a) potential GDPR implications for real employees, and (b) the risk of inadvertently modifying live records. We replicate a realistic scenario — the learning outcome is the same."
