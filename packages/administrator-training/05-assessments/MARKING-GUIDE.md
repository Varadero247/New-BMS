## Part A Marking (MCQ)

- **1 mark** for correct answer
- **0 marks** for incorrect or blank
- No negative marking
- No partial credit

Use the ANSWER-KEY.md for the correct answer to each question.

**Common marking situations:**

If a participant circles two answers: 0 marks (only one correct answer permitted).
If a participant crosses out one answer and circles another: mark the final circled answer.
If answer is unclear: ask the participant to clarify before marking. If clarification impossible: 0 marks.

---

## Part B Marking (Scenarios)

Each scenario is worth **5 marks**. Each mark corresponds to a specific criterion in the ANSWER-KEY.md.

### Marking Principles

**Award full marks when:**
The response demonstrates a correct understanding of the concept, even if the exact wording differs from the answer key. Look for conceptual accuracy, not rote repetition.

**Award partial marks (0.5) when:**
The response is partially correct — captures the main idea but misses a detail, or includes the correct action but with a minor error in sequence.

**Award 0 marks when:**
The response is incorrect, contradicts platform behaviour, or demonstrates a misunderstanding of the concept.

### Scenario-Specific Notes

**Scenario A (SCIM Outage):**
Participants who identify multiple possible root causes (token vs. mapping vs. connectivity) and outline diagnostic steps for each should receive full marks for the diagnostic criterion even if they don't identify the "most likely" cause.

**Scenario B (Privilege Escalation):**
The data protection notification criterion (mark 4) is often underweighted. Accept: "notify data protection officer", "check GDPR obligations", or any specific correct reference to 72-hour notification window.

**Scenario C (Failed Update Rollback):**
Accept manual rollback via CLI if participant knows the equivalent command sequence, even if they don't mention the admin console path.

## Time Management for Facilitator

| Activity | Time allocation |
|---------|----------------|
| Part A auto-mark (portal) or tally (printed) | 10 min (during Part B) |
| Part B marking (all 3 scenarios × all participants) | 20–30 min |
| Total marking time | 30–40 min |

**Tip**: If group size > 8, ask a co-facilitator to mark Part B for half the participants.

## Notifying Results

Results should be:
1. Calculated to the nearest whole mark
2. Announced individually (not publicly ranked)
3. Recorded in the Nexara Training Registry by the facilitator
4. Accompanied by a printed or portal-generated result slip

**Result slip format:**
```
Nexara IMS Administrator Training — Assessment Result

Name: [Full name]
Organisation: [Org name]
Date: [Assessment date]
Part A: [X]/40
Part B: [Y]/15
Total: [Z]/55 ([%]%)
Grade: [Pass / Pass with Distinction / Fail]

Next steps:
[If Pass/Distinction]: Certificate issued. Training Registry ref: [UUID]
[If Fail]: Retake available at next cohort. Contact: training@nexara.io
```

---

## Retake Policy

- Participants who score < 75% may retake at the next scheduled cohort
- No mandatory waiting period
- Participants retaking should review the modules where they lost marks
- Retake assessment uses a different question set (facilitator: request from Nexara Training Team)
- A participant may take the assessment a maximum of 3 times. Further attempts require additional training.

---

## Competency Checklist Sign-Off

The **COMPETENCY-CHECKLIST.md** is used by the facilitator during lab sessions (not the assessment) to sign off observable behaviours. Competency sign-off is separate from the assessment mark and should be completed throughout Day 1 and Day 2 labs.

Lab sign-off does not affect the certificate grade but is logged in the Training Registry as evidence of practical competency.
