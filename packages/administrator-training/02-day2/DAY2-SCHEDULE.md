## Session Breakdowns

### 08:30–09:00 — Day 1 Recap & Day 2 Objectives (30 min)

**Facilitator actions:**
- 08:30–08:40: "What's one thing from yesterday you've already thought about applying?" (round-table, 1 sentence each)
- 08:40–08:50: Rapid-fire recap of Day 1 key concepts (facilitator-led quiz, verbal answers)
- 08:50–09:00: Day 2 objectives and assessment overview ("This afternoon you sit the summative — here's what to expect")

**Energy note**: Energy varies (some participants slept on it, others are anxious about the assessment). Normalise the assessment early: "The assessment is the beginning of your journey as a certified admin, not the end."

---

### 09:00–10:30 — Module 5: Audit Log Review (90 min)

**Structure:**
| Time | Activity | Format |
|------|----------|--------|
| 09:00–09:20 | Audit architecture and event taxonomy | Presentation |
| 09:20–09:45 | Live demo: filtering, searching, exporting | Demo |
| 09:45–10:15 | **LAB-05**: Mock incident investigation | Hands-on |
| 10:15–10:30 | Debrief: what did you find? | Discussion |

**Content focus**: Audit architecture, 5 event categories, 47 event types, filtering, tamper-evidence, export formats.

**Energy note**: LAB-05 (mock investigation) is the most engaging lab of the two days. Play it up: "You're the investigator. Someone escalated their own privileges. Find them."

---

### 10:30–10:45 — Morning Break

Encourage participants to think through the investigation scenario: "Did anyone find something unexpected?"

---

### 10:45–12:15 — Module 6: Backup & Restore Procedures (90 min)

**Structure:**
| Time | Activity | Format |
|------|----------|--------|
| 10:45–11:05 | Backup architecture overview | Presentation |
| 11:05–11:20 | pg_dump live demo + checksum verification | Demo |
| 11:20–11:55 | **LAB-06**: Backup + restore sequence | Hands-on |
| 11:55–12:15 | DR runbook walkthrough + Q&A | Discussion |

**Content focus**: pg_dump/pg_restore, WAL archiving, backup schedules, retention tiers, restore procedures, DR runbook.

**Energy note**: Procedural module; keep it hands-on. The moment of completing a restore successfully is a satisfying milestone for participants.

---

### 12:15–13:00 — Lunch

**Facilitator**: Lighter conversation today. Participants will be thinking about the assessment. Brief reassurance is appropriate but don't dwell on it.

---

### 13:00–14:00 — Module 7: Platform Update Management (60 min)

**Structure:**
| Time | Activity | Format |
|------|----------|--------|
| 13:00–13:20 | Update lifecycle and gate criteria | Presentation |
| 13:20–13:35 | Pre-update checklist walkthrough | Demo |
| 13:35–13:50 | **LAB-07**: Update plan + rollback scenario | Hands-on |
| 13:50–14:00 | Feature flags demo + final Q&A | Demo |

**Content focus**: Update types, staging gates, pre-update checklist, rollback triggers, feature flags.

**Energy note**: Keep Module 7 tight. 60 minutes is intentionally short. Leave the last 10 minutes open for any lingering questions from Day 1 or Day 2 before the assessment.

---

### 14:00–14:15 — Afternoon Break

**Facilitator**: Don't discuss assessment content. Encourage participants to use the break to clear their head.

---

### 14:15–15:15 — Summative Assessment (60 min)

**Structure:**
| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 14:15–14:20 | Instructions (40 MCQ + 3 scenarios; 60 min total) |
| 14:20–15:05 | Part A: 40 MCQ (45 minutes) |
| 15:05–15:15 | Part B: 3 scenario questions (reading time; written responses) |

**Administration rules:**
- Silence throughout
- No collaboration
- Portal or printed paper — confirm before assessment
- Participants may not leave during assessment (virtual: honour system)
- Participants who finish Part A early may begin Part B reading time

**Facilitator (during Part B)**: Begin marking Part A using the answer key while participants work on Part B.

---

### 15:15–15:45 — Assessment Debrief + Marking (30 min)

**Facilitator actions:**
- 15:15–15:30: Collect Part B responses; mark Part A with group ("Let's go through every question you got wrong")
- 15:30–15:45: Mark Part B responses (facilitator); announce results

**Announcing results:**
"I'm going to call each person individually. Your score, your grade, and your certificate status. Everything is confidential — I'll give you a slip with your results."

**If a participant fails:**
"You've covered excellent ground these two days. A retake is available at the next cohort — [date if known]. This is not a reflection of your capability; it's a gap we'll close together."

---

### 15:45–16:15 — Action Planning, Next Steps, Q&A (30 min)

**Facilitator prompt**: "Before we close, take 5 minutes individually: write three actions you'll take in your organisation within the next 30 days as a result of this training."

**Round-table sharing**: Each participant shares one action. Facilitator captures on whiteboard.

**Next steps information:**
- Certificate registration: Nexara Training Registry (7-year retention)
- CPD claim: download CPD certificate from training portal
- Support: admin support email/helpdesk contact
- Advanced training: Nexara Advanced Administrator (if available)
- Post-training resources: IMS admin guide, changelog, community forum

---

### 16:15–16:30 — Certificate Ceremony (15 min)

"It's my pleasure to award the following certificates today."

**[Call each participant by name. Announce their grade: Pass / Pass with Distinction.]**

"[Name], Nexara Certified Platform Administrator [with Distinction]. Congratulations."

**Group photo opportunity** (virtual: screenshot of all participants with certificates on screen).

**Facilitator close:**
"You came here as platform users. You're leaving as certified platform administrators. The organisation you work for is more resilient because you're in this room. Congratulations to all of you."
