# Virtual Delivery Guide — Nexara IMS Administrator Training

---

## Platform Requirements

**Primary**: Microsoft Teams or Zoom (both fully supported)
**Backup**: Google Meet (functionality reduced — no breakout rooms in free tier)

### Facilitator Technical Setup

| Item | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| Camera | HD webcam; ensure face is well-lit (avoid backlight) |
| Microphone | Headset or dedicated microphone (not laptop mic) |
| Second monitor | Strongly recommended: slides on one screen, participant view on other |
| Internet | Wired connection; minimum 10 Mbps upload |
| Browser | Chrome for screen sharing; keep IMS admin console tabs pre-loaded |
| Backup | Mobile hotspot ready; phone number for co-facilitator |

---

## Pre-Session Setup (1 hour before)

### Meeting Configuration

**Zoom:**
- Enable Waiting Room (admit participants individually)
- Pre-create breakout rooms (1 room per 2 participants for group exercises)
- Enable Polls (pre-load any polls from slide materials)
- Disable "Allow participants to unmute themselves" during assessments

**Teams:**
- Create meeting with Teams Premium if available (breakout rooms, advanced polling)
- Pre-create Breakout Rooms: name them by lab (LAB-01 Group A, LAB-01 Group B)
- Enable Together Mode for introductions (optional; improves social presence)

### Chat Rules (post at session start)

> "Chat rules for today:
> ✓ Use chat to ask questions any time — I check it regularly
> ✓ Use reactions (👍❓👏) to signal understanding or confusion
> ✓ Introduce yourself in chat: name + organisation + one thing you administer
> ✗ Please avoid side conversations in DMs — if it's relevant, share with the group
> ✗ Mute when not speaking"

## Adapted Agenda for Virtual Delivery

| Element | In-Person | Virtual Adaptation |
|---------|----------|-------------------|
| Introductions | Round-table | Chat introductions + verbal spotlight (2 per min) |
| Lab exercises | Individual at laptop | Individual: share screen with group; pair: breakout rooms |
| Group exercises (LAB-02) | Sticky notes + whiteboard | Breakout rooms; collaborative Google Doc or Miro board |
| Facilitated debrief | Standing at front | Gallery view; use "raise hand" to manage turn-taking |
| Assessment (formative + summative) | Printed paper / console | Training portal; honour system; no breakout rooms |
| Certificate ceremony | Physical handover | Screenshot of certificate in portal; group Zoom photo |

## Managing Engagement Virtually

### Attention Signals

Check engagement every 20 minutes:
- "Give me a thumbs up in reactions if you're following OK"
- "Type '1' in chat if you're ready to move on; '2' if you need more time"
- "Who'd like to share their screen and show us their lab progress?"

### Re-Engaging Disengaged Participants

- Call on participants by name with a gentle question: "Alex, what would you do first in this scenario?"
- Ask for chat responses: "Everyone: type your answer in chat before I reveal it"
- Use polls (pre-load 2–3 polls per day for key concepts)

### Managing the Chat

Assign a co-facilitator (or experienced participant) to monitor chat and flag questions. If solo:
- Check chat during transitions; not mid-explanation
- Acknowledge: "Great question from Sarah in chat — let me address that now"
- Park complex questions: "Good one — I'll come back to that in the lab debrief"

## Breakout Rooms (LAB-02, Group Exercises)

### Setup (Zoom)

1. Pre-create rooms: "LAB-02 Group A", "LAB-02 Group B", etc.
2. Assign 3–4 participants per room
3. Broadcast a timer message to all rooms: "5 minutes remaining"
4. Visit each room briefly (2 min each) to observe progress

### Setup (Teams)

1. Facilitator opens Breakout Rooms panel
2. Auto-assign participants randomly or manually
3. Set timer: 20–25 minutes
4. Broadcast message at 5 min warning
5. Close rooms to return all participants to main session

### Providing Instruction in Breakout Rooms

Before sending to rooms:
- State the task clearly
- Share the written brief (paste into chat)
- Specify the output: "Come back with 3 role assignment decisions and your rationale"
- Confirm time: "You have 25 minutes. I'll visit each room."

## Virtual Assessment Administration

### Pre-Assessment & Day 1 Formative

- Direct participants to training portal assessment pages
- Set a timer visible to all (share your screen with timer)
- Announce: "Work individually. Close any other tabs. You have 15 minutes."
- During assessment: do not chat/speak. Monitor time only.

### Summative Assessment

**Part A (40 MCQ, 45 min):**
- Training portal handles timing automatically
- Request: "Please close all other browser tabs and have only the training portal open"
- Honour system for virtual delivery
- At 45 min: announce "Part A complete. Move to Part B."

**Part B (3 scenarios, 15 min):**
- Options:
  1. Training portal scenario submission (preferred)
  2. Share a Google Form link (copy responses for marking)
  3. Email responses to facilitator within 5 minutes of time-up

**Marking (during Part B):**
If using training portal: Part A is auto-marked. Facilitator marks Part B responses.
If printed / emailed: Mark Part A from answer key during Part B.

Direct each participant to:
**Training Portal → Certificate → Download**

Take a group screenshot: "Everyone, please display your certificate on screen — let's capture this moment."

## Common Virtual Issues & Fixes

| Issue | Fix |
|-------|-----|
| Participant audio echo | Ask them to use headset or join from different device |
| Screen share lag | Ask sharer to reduce display resolution to 720p; close other tabs |
| Participant dropped out | Continue; they can rejoin; share the recording link |
| Breakout room not working | Use chat groups instead; each group posts to a named thread |
| Training portal not loading | Check browser; clear cache; try incognito; use mobile as fallback |
| Co-facilitator absent | Slow the pace slightly; rely more on participant engagement |
| Tech failure during demo | Switch to screenshot slides; resume demo when resolved |

## Recording Policy

Virtual sessions may be recorded with participant consent:
1. Announce at session start: "This session will be recorded for internal review and for participants who need to revisit content. Do you all consent?"
2. If any participant declines: do not record.
3. Recording shared with participants only; not used for marketing without explicit consent.
4. Delete recording after 6 months unless participant requests otherwise.
