# Facilitation Troubleshooting — Common Issues & Resolutions

---

## Participant Issues

### Late Joiners

**Situation**: A participant arrives 30–60 minutes into Day 1.

**Response:**
1. Acknowledge privately during the next break: "Welcome — glad you made it. We've covered [X]. Your handbook has the full content; I'll catch you up in the break."
2. Do NOT restart or significantly recap for one late joiner — this penalises punctual participants.
3. During lunch or break, give a 5-minute briefing on what was missed.
4. Note in your register what content they missed for assessment purposes.

**If a participant misses > 3 hours of the programme:**
- Advise they reschedule to the next available cohort.
- They are not eligible to sit the summative assessment.

---

### Participant Dominating Discussion

**Situation**: One participant answers every question, preventing others from contributing.

**Response techniques:**
- "Great answer — now I'd love to hear from someone who hasn't spoken yet. [Name]?"
- Structured turns: "Let's go around the table — I'd like one answer from each person."
- Written answers before verbal: "Write your answer in the handbook first, then share."
- During group exercises: assign roles (recorder, presenter) so dominant participants don't default to presenter every time.

---

### Participant Disengaged or on Phone

**Situation**: Participant frequently checking phone; visibly disengaged.

**Response:**
- Avoid calling out publicly. Target a question at them during a natural pause: "Alex, what would you add to that?"
- During break: "Are you getting what you need from today? Is there anything I can adjust?"
- If persistent: private conversation at lunch. "I've noticed you seem distracted — is everything OK? Is there anything preventing you from engaging with the programme?"
- Rarely, a participant has a work emergency. Allow them to step out; provide catch-up plan.

---

### Participant Significantly Struggling

**Situation**: A participant is visibly struggling with the technical content; failing lab exercises; frustrated.

**Response:**
- Never single them out publicly.
- During a lab: approach quietly. "How's it going? Where are you stuck?" Listen before offering solutions.
- Pair with a stronger participant for the next exercise (frame as peer learning, not remediation).
- At lunch or break: private check-in. Discuss additional support options.
- Post-Day 1 formative: if score < 40%, have an honest conversation about whether Day 2 assessment preparation is realistic. Suggest retake and additional study materials.

---

### Participant Who "Already Knows All This"

**Situation**: Experienced participant signals they know the material; may disengage or undermine.

**Response:**
- Engage them as expert peer: "You've clearly got experience here — I'd love for you to share with the group."
- Give them extension activities in labs.
- During debrief: ask them to surface nuances the group may have missed.
- Privately: "Your experience is valuable — the others are learning a lot from your contributions."

## Technical Issues

### Lab Environment Down

**Situation**: Training sandbox is inaccessible (HTTP 503, login failure).

**Immediate response:**
1. Don't panic. Stay calm — this is a teachable moment.
2. Ask participants to close the browser and retry in 60 seconds.
3. Contact Nexara Support: `support@nexara.io` or emergency hotline (provided in your facilitator pack).
4. While waiting: continue with the module presentation slides.

**If > 10 minutes down:**
- Switch to scenario-based mode (LAB files have a scenario-based path).
- Use screenshots from participant handbook to demonstrate procedures.
- Note the time lost; adjust remaining lab times proportionally.

---

### Facilitator Laptop Fails

**Situation**: Facilitator's laptop crashes, loses connection, or won't project.

**Preparation (always have):**
- Slides in cloud storage (OneDrive, Google Drive) — accessible from any device
- Slides emailed to yourself and co-facilitator as backup
- Printed facilitation guide to work from without slides
- Participant handbook contains all key content

**Response:**
1. If projector: use participant's laptop connected to projector, sharing your slides from cloud
2. If total loss: facilitate from the whiteboard + participant handbooks
3. For demos: ask a participant to share their screen and drive the demo while you narrate

---

### Admin Console Demo Fails (Live)

**Situation**: During a live demo, the admin console returns an error or behaves unexpectedly.

**Response:**
- "Perfect timing — this is exactly the kind of error we'll cover in Module [X]."
- If recoverable in < 30 seconds: fix it live; narrate what you're doing.
- If not recoverable: "Let me show you what this should look like" — switch to screenshot slide.
- Note the issue; report to Nexara Support after the session.

## Pacing Issues

### Running Behind Schedule

**Situation**: It is 12:00 and you have not finished Module 2.

**Response:**
1. Identify what can be cut: appendix content, extended examples, optional Q&A
2. "I'm going to flag the next section as self-study — it's fully covered in your handbook, page [X]."
3. Do not rush through content — skipping clearly is better than rushing unclearly.
4. Adjust break times slightly (shorten by 5 min) if needed, but never eliminate breaks.
5. Communicate to participants: "We're running slightly behind, so I'm going to prioritise the most important content and flag the rest as reading."

**Never sacrifice:**
- Lab time (hands-on learning is irreplaceable)
- Assessment time
- Certificate ceremony

---

### Running Ahead of Schedule

**Situation**: You've finished Module 3 with 20 minutes to spare.

**Response:**
1. Extend the lab with the live extension activity.
2. Open for Q&A on the content covered so far.
3. Do a preview of Module 4 to build anticipation.
4. Do NOT start Module 4 early if it would bleed into the break — keep to schedule.

---

## Energy Issues

### Post-Lunch Energy Crash

**Situation**: Energy is visibly low after lunch — people are passive, slow to respond.

**Techniques:**
- Stand-up activity: "Stretch break — everyone stand up. Name one thing from this morning."
- Quick-fire round: rapid verbal questions with short answers — no thinking time, just responses.
- Hands-on activity immediately: don't start a slide presentation right after lunch.
- Ask participants to share something they'd apply in their org: creates relevance.

---

### Assessment Anxiety (Day 2)

**Situation**: Participants are visibly anxious about the summative assessment.

**Response:**
- Normalise at the start of Day 2: "The assessment is designed to confirm what you've learned, not trick you."
- Remind during Module 7 close: "Everything in the assessment was covered in these two days. No surprises."
- Before distributing assessment: "Take a breath. You're ready."
- Do NOT share questions in advance or hint at specific topics — this undermines the certificate's integrity.

---

## Assessment Issues

### Participant Requests Extra Time

**Situation**: A participant asks for additional time on the summative.

**Response:**
- If pre-disclosed disability requiring accommodation: honour the accommodation (pre-agreed with client)
- If unexpected: a standard extension of 10 minutes is permitted for documented reasons
- Note any extensions in your facilitator debrief report

---

### Suspected Collaboration During Assessment

**Situation**: You observe or suspect two participants sharing answers.

**Response (in-person):**
1. Quietly separate the participants if room allows.
2. Make a note of the observed behaviour.
3. Do not accuse publicly.
4. After assessment: speak to both individually. If confirmed: results for both are withheld pending Nexara Training Team review.

**Response (virtual):**
1. Note the time and participants.
2. After assessment: contact Nexara Training Team with your observation.
3. Results held pending review.
