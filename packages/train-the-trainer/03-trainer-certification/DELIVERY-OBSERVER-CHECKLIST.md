## Scoring Scale

| Score | Descriptor |
|-------|-----------|
| **4** | **Exemplary** — Consistently exceeds the standard; evidence of best practice across the full delivery |
| **3** | **Proficient** — Meets the standard consistently; minor areas for improvement only |
| **2** | **Developing** — Meets the standard inconsistently; specific development actions required |
| **1** | **Insufficient** — Does not meet the standard; significant development required before delivery |

## Pass Criteria

| Threshold | Requirement |
|-----------|------------|
| Overall average | ≥ 2.5 (equivalent to 70% of 4-point maximum) |
| Competency 1 — Content Mastery | Minimum score: **2** |
| Competency 2 — Facilitation | Minimum score: **2** |
| Competency 3 — Assessment Administration | Minimum score: **2** *(observation of preparation and setup only; not assessed in the 20-min delivery itself)* |

| Competency 5 — Professionalism | Minimum score: **2** |

**Both criteria must be met**: overall average ≥ 2.5 AND all domain minimums met.

## Competency 1: Content Mastery

*Is the content accurate, contextualised, and delivered with demonstrable understanding?*

### Observable Evidence (record 2–3 specific observations during delivery)

| # | Time | Observation |
|---|------|-------------|
| 1 | | |
| 2 | | |

### Evidence Descriptors

**Score 4 — Exemplary**
- [ ] All content delivered without factual errors throughout the 20 minutes
- [ ] Content is enriched with relevant, contextualised examples drawn from audience's likely experience
- [ ] Common misconceptions are proactively addressed (not just reactive to participant questions)
- [ ] Unexpected content questions answered correctly and confidently; uncertain areas explicitly flagged with commitment to follow up
- [ ] Demonstrates mastery of the regulatory/ISO framework underlying the Nexara content

**Score 3 — Proficient**
- [ ] All content is accurate; no material factual errors
- [ ] Examples connect relevantly to participant roles
- [ ] Most content questions handled competently; one or two complex queries deferred appropriately
- [ ] Understanding of compliance rationale demonstrated alongside system navigation

**Score 2 — Developing**
- [ ] Content substantially accurate; one or two minor inaccuracies
- [ ] Examples are generic, not contextualised to audience
- [ ] Off-script questions cause uncertainty; one or more answers are incorrect or over-qualified
- [ ] "What" of content understood; "why" not consistently demonstrated

**Score 1 — Insufficient**
- [ ] Material factual errors that would mislead participants
- [ ] Cannot explain compliance rationale without reading from materials
- [ ] Basic content questions cannot be answered without script; participant questions go unanswered

## Competency 2: Facilitation

*Is an effective learning environment created, with appropriate instructional strategies, group management, and participant engagement?*

### Observable Evidence (record 2–3 specific observations during delivery)

| # | Time | Observation |
|---|------|-------------|
| 1 | | |
| 2 | | |
| 3 | | |

### Evidence Descriptors

**Score 4 — Exemplary**
- [ ] Minimum 4 different instructional strategies used (e.g. lecture, demonstration, Q&A, pair work, sandbox, case study)
- [ ] Group energy actively managed — pace shifts, activity changes, or participant redirection used in response to energy signals
- [ ] Quieter participants drawn out without discomfort; dominant voices managed constructively
- [ ] Session transitions are seamless and purposeful
- [ ] Wait time used effectively after questions; silence is not filled immediately

**Score 3 — Proficient**
- [ ] Minimum 3 instructional strategies used
- [ ] Session energy generally maintained; trainer notices and responds to drops
- [ ] Open questions predominate in discussion segments; closed questions used appropriately
- [ ] Transitions are clear; agenda is tracked
- [ ] Participant contributions acknowledged and built upon

**Score 2 — Developing**
- [ ] One or two strategies only (typically lecture + Q&A)
- [ ] Energy fluctuates without trainer adaptation
- [ ] Questions tend to be closed; discussion opportunities are underused
- [ ] Some transitions are abrupt or not signposted
- [ ] Group dynamic management is reactive rather than proactive

**Score 1 — Insufficient**
- [ ] Reads from materials for the majority of the delivery; no interaction strategy
- [ ] No response to energy signals; flat delivery throughout
- [ ] No open questions; participants never invited to engage

### Evidence Descriptors

**Score 4 — Exemplary**
- [ ] Pre-session materials are perfectly organised; participant-facing and facilitator materials clearly separated
- [ ] Demonstrates knowledge of assessment timing and invigilation requirements in response to any assessment-related question during delivery
- [ ] Assessment files (if referenced) are opened and used correctly; answer keys not visible

- [ ] Assessment-related questions (if arising) handled correctly

## Competency 4: Programme Management

*Does the trainer manage the session administration effectively, including preparation, timing, and logistics?*

### Observable Evidence

| # | Time | Observation |
|---|------|-------------|
| 1 | | |
| 2 | | |

### Evidence Descriptors

**Score 4 — Exemplary**
- [ ] Delivery begins on time; all materials and technology are ready
- [ ] Time management is precise — pacing through the 20-minute segment is deliberate; no rushing or padding
- [ ] Unexpected logistical issues (AV glitch, question causing a tangent) managed without losing the audience
- [ ] Demonstrates knowledge of version control and reporting obligations in response to any programme management questions

**Score 3 — Proficient**
- [ ] Session starts on time; materials are prepared
- [ ] Time management is good; minor adjustments to stay within 20 minutes
- [ ] Common logistical issues handled without disruption

**Score 2 — Developing**
- [ ] Minor preparation gaps (material not immediately to hand; slight late start)
- [ ] Time management issues — segment either runs significantly over or finishes more than 3 minutes early
- [ ] Some logistical issues cause participant-visible delays

**Score 1 — Insufficient**
- [ ] Significant preparation failures — session delayed or unable to start as planned
- [ ] Time management failure causes substantial content coverage gap or significant overrun

## Competency 5: Professionalism

*Does the trainer present to the standard expected of a Nexara representative: presentation, communication style, ethics, and adherence to brand standards?*

### Observable Evidence

| # | Time | Observation |
|---|------|-------------|
| 1 | | |
| 2 | | |

### Evidence Descriptors

**Score 4 — Exemplary**
- [ ] Personal presentation is impeccable
- [ ] All materials are co-branded correctly (client logo in co-branding zone; Nexara logo visible; no prohibited modifications)
- [ ] Communication style is inclusive, respectful, and consistently engaging
- [ ] Demonstrates empathy in any challenging participant interaction
- [ ] Actively frames the value of training beyond the compliance requirement
- [ ] Session starts and ends within the time window; no significant overrun

**Score 3 — Proficient**
- [ ] Presentation is professional
- [ ] Materials meet co-branding standards
- [ ] Communication is clear and respectful throughout
- [ ] Challenging interactions handled constructively
- [ ] Time management is good; minor overruns self-corrected

**Score 2 — Developing**
- [ ] Presentation is acceptable; minor co-branding deviation noted (not a core IP violation)
- [ ] Communication style is generally professional but inconsistent in tone
- [ ] Some difficulty managing a challenging participant interaction
- [ ] One time management issue (overrun or significant early finish)

**Score 1 — Insufficient**
- [ ] Presentation below professional standard
- [ ] Co-branding violation that breaches Nexara IP requirements
- [ ] Communication style that excludes, demeans, or alienates participants
- [ ] Significant time management failure affecting content coverage
