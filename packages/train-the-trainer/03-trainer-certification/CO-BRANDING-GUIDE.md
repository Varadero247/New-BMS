# Co-Branding Guide — Train-the-Trainer Certified Trainers

**Programme**: Nexara IMS Train-the-Trainer (T3)
**Version**: 1.0
**Applies to**: All certified internal trainers delivering End User and Module Owner Training programmes

---

## 1. Purpose

This guide defines the rules for co-branding Nexara training materials with your organisation's identity. These rules protect Nexara's intellectual property, maintain consistent programme quality standards, and ensure participants receive materials that are clearly identified as Nexara-certified content.

Co-branding rights are granted to certified internal trainers upon receiving the "Nexara Certified Internal Trainer" credential. These rights are non-transferable and expire if the credential lapses.

---

## 2. The Co-Branding Principle

The fundamental co-branding principle is:

> **Nexara is always visible. Your organisation's identity is always additive, never substitutive.**

Your organisation's logo and name can appear alongside Nexara's — but never in place of it, never at the expense of Nexara's visibility, and never in a way that creates ambiguity about who produced the training content.

---

## 3. Permitted Co-Branding Modifications

### 3.1 Digital Slides and Screen Presentations

| Element | Permitted Modification |
|---------|----------------------|
| Slide header | Add your organisation logo in the top-right corner (Nexara logo remains top-left) |
| Slide footer | Add "Delivered by [Organisation] L&D" text in the footer alongside "Nexara IMS Training" text |
| Cover slide | Add co-brand line: "Nexara IMS Training — Delivered by [Organisation]"; maintain the standard Nexara cover design |
| Colour accents | Do not change the Nexara navy/gold colour scheme; you may add your organisation's brand colour as a secondary accent on supplementary slides (additional case studies, org-specific content) |

**Prohibited slide changes**:
- Removing or resizing the Nexara logo
- Replacing the Nexara navy/gold colour scheme with your organisation's brand palette
- Adding your organisation's logo where the Nexara logo appears (main position)
- Editing the programme title to remove "Nexara IMS"

### 3.2 Printed Participant Handouts and Workbooks

| Element | Permitted Modification |
|---------|----------------------|
| Cover page | Client logo in the top-right co-branding zone (see diagram in `CERTIFICATE-SPEC.md`) |
| Running footer | "Powered by Nexara IMS — Delivered by [Organisation]" |
| Header | Organisation name may be added after the Nexara programme name (e.g. "Nexara IMS End User Training — [Organisation] Cohort, April 2026") |

**Prohibited printed changes**:
- Removing the Nexara copyright notice from the final page of handouts
- Adding your organisation's copyright claim to Nexara content
- Reprinting Nexara materials with your organisation's sole branding

### 3.3 Email Communications to Participants

| Element | Permitted Modification |
|---------|----------------------|
| Email sender name | "[Organisation] L&D" is acceptable |
| Email subject | May use "[Organisation] Training — [Programme Name]" — Nexara name in the programme name is sufficient |
| Email body | May include your organisation's standard email template header/footer; include Nexara programme name and sandbox credentials |
| Pre-programme invitation | May be sent from your HR/L&D system rather than the Nexara portal auto-mailer — retain all required content fields |

### 3.4 Venue and Room Branding (In-Person Delivery)

| Element | Permitted |
|---------|----------|
| Room signage | "Nexara IMS Training — [Organisation] Cohort" ✓ |
| Room signage | "[Organisation] Training Day" (no Nexara reference) ✗ |
| Pull-up banners | Client banner in the room alongside any Nexara-provided materials ✓ |
| Nexara-produced materials (workbooks, certificates) | Must be used as issued; do not substitute with reprinted versions |

## 4. Prohibited Modifications — Detailed

### 4.1 White-Labelling

White-labelling is the removal of all Nexara branding and the substitution of the client's brand identity, creating the impression that the training programme is the client's own proprietary product.

**This is strictly prohibited and constitutes a licence violation.**

Prohibited white-labelling examples:
- Removing all Nexara references from slide decks and replacing with your organisation name
- Issuing certificates that do not include the Nexara name
- Publishing the training on your internal LMS under a name that omits Nexara
- Describing the programme to participants as "our compliance training" without reference to Nexara

**Consequence**: Discovery of white-labelling will result in immediate suspension of the training licence and potential legal action for IP infringement.

### 4.2 Content Attribution Removal

The following attribution statements must appear on all participant-distributed materials:

- On the first or last page of any printed handout: *"This training programme is developed and owned by Nexara DMCC. Delivered under licence by [Organisation]."*
- On digital slide decks: equivalent notice in the slide notes or on the closing slide

Removing or paraphrasing this attribution in a way that obscures Nexara's ownership is prohibited.

### 4.3 Logo Size Violations

Your organisation logo in the co-branding zone must not:
- Exceed the Nexara logo in overall visual weight (approximate size parity is acceptable; significantly larger is prohibited)
- Be placed in a position that creates visual precedence over the Nexara logo
- Replace the Nexara logo on certificates

### 4.4 Unapproved Language Changes

The following programme names are protected and may not be altered:

| Protected Name | Example Permitted Usage | Example Prohibited Usage |
|---------------|------------------------|------------------------|
| "Nexara Certified Internal Trainer" | "Nexara Certified Internal Trainer — [Org] Cohort" | "XYZ Certified Compliance Trainer" |
| "Nexara Platform Foundation" | "Nexara Platform Foundation — Delivered by [Org]" | "XYZ Digital Compliance Programme" |
| "Nexara Certified Module Owner — [Day]" | Unchanged; [Org] name may appear in subtitle only | "XYZ Quality Management Certificate" |

## 5. Supplementary Client Content

Certified internal trainers may add supplementary content (additional case studies, organisation-specific scenarios, internal policy references) to their delivery, subject to the following rules:

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Clearly demarcated | Supplementary content must be clearly distinguished from Nexara source material (e.g. different slide background, clearly labelled "Organisation Case Study") |
| Non-contradictory | Supplementary content must not contradict Nexara guidance or create conflicting instructions |
| Non-substitutive | Supplementary content augments; it does not replace Nexara content modules |
| Consistent with platform | Supplementary content must accurately describe Nexara IMS functionality; incorrect supplementary content is a compliance risk |

---

## 6. Reporting a Co-Branding Query

If you are uncertain whether a proposed modification is within the permitted scope:

1. Email training@nexara.io with the subject: "Co-Branding Query — [Organisation]"
2. Describe the proposed change and attach an example if available
3. Nexara L&D will confirm within **2 business days** whether the modification is permitted

Do not implement uncertain modifications pending approval. If you have already implemented a modification and discover it may be prohibited, contact Nexara immediately — proactive disclosure is treated more favourably than discovered violations.

---

## 7. Co-Branding in the Training Portal

When you set up your co-branding in the Training Portal:

1. Log in with trainer credentials
2. Navigate to **Settings → Organisation Branding**
3. Upload your organisation logo (PNG or SVG; recommended: white or light version for dark backgrounds)
4. Enter the co-brand display name (e.g. "Meridian Industrial Group L&D")
5. Save — this applies automatically to all certificates and portal pages for your cohorts

The Training Portal enforces the Nexara logo position and size programmatically — you cannot accidentally place your logo in the Nexara logo zone via the portal settings. However, manual co-branding of printed materials (slide decks, handouts) is your responsibility and is governed by this guide.
