# Train-the-Trainer — Competency Framework

**Programme**: Nexara IMS Train-the-Trainer (T3)
**Version**: 1.0
**Purpose**: Defines the five observable trainer competencies assessed during the programme and used for annual renewal review

---

## Framework Overview

The Nexara Internal Trainer Competency Framework defines five domains of trainer capability. These domains are assessed during the **observed delivery assessment** (Day 2 Lab) using the rubric in `03-trainer-certification/DELIVERY-OBSERVER-CHECKLIST.md`. Each domain is scored on a 4-point scale:

| Score | Descriptor |
|-------|-----------|
| 4 | **Exemplary** — Consistently exceeds the standard; evidence of best practice; could mentor others |
| 3 | **Proficient** — Meets the standard consistently; only minor areas for improvement |
| 2 | **Developing** — Meets the standard inconsistently; specific development actions identified |
| 1 | **Insufficient** — Does not meet the standard; significant development required before delivery |

**Minimum domain scores** for certification:

| Competency Domain | Minimum Score | Rationale |
|------------------|--------------|-----------|
| 1. Content Mastery | 2 | Inaccurate content delivery is a compliance risk |
| 2. Facilitation | 2 | Below-standard facilitation undermines participant learning |
| 3. Assessment Administration | 2 | Assessment integrity is non-negotiable |
| 4. Programme Management | 1 | Partly assessed via written assessment; minimum acceptable |
| 5. Professionalism | 2 | Represents Nexara brand to client participants |
| **Overall average** | **≥ 2.5 (70%)** | Must be met in addition to domain minimums |

## Competency 1: Content Mastery

**Definition**: The trainer accurately and confidently represents Nexara IMS content and the underlying regulatory/standards framework. They can contextualise content for participant roles and respond to content questions without misrepresentation.

### Observable Evidence Descriptors

**Score 4 — Exemplary**
- Delivers all content accurately with no factual errors
- Enriches Nexara content with relevant, contextualised examples drawn from participants' industry or role
- Anticipates common misconceptions and proactively addresses them before participants surface them
- Answers unexpected content questions correctly and confidently; flags genuinely uncertain areas with a commitment to follow up
- Demonstrates mastery of the ISO/regulatory framework underlying the content

**Score 3 — Proficient**
- Delivers all content accurately; no material factual errors
- Provides relevant examples that connect to participants' roles
- Handles most content questions competently; may defer one or two complex questions
- Demonstrates solid understanding of why compliance requirements exist, not just what they are

**Score 2 — Developing**
- Content is substantially accurate but one or two minor inaccuracies are present
- Examples are generic (not contextualised to audience)
- Struggles with off-script questions; may give incorrect or uncertain responses
- Understands the "what" of content but not consistently the "why"

**Score 1 — Insufficient**
- Material factual errors that could mislead participants
- Cannot explain the rationale behind compliance requirements
- Unable to answer basic content questions without reading directly from materials
- Relies entirely on scripts without demonstrating understanding

### Development Actions for Score 2

- Complete one additional Nexara Module Owner programme in the relevant subject area
- Shadow a Nexara Master Trainer for one full-day delivery session
- Complete the self-study reading list in `01-day-1-foundations/MODULE-NEXARA-CURRICULUM.md`

## Competency 2: Facilitation

**Definition**: The trainer creates an effective learning environment — managing group dynamics, pacing the session, using a range of instructional strategies, and responding adaptively to participant needs and energy levels.

### Observable Evidence Descriptors

**Score 4 — Exemplary**
- Uses at least four different instructional strategies within the session (e.g. lecture, Q&A, case study, pair discussion, demonstration)
- Actively manages group energy — shifts pace, adds movement or group tasks when energy drops
- Draws out quieter participants without creating discomfort; manages dominant voices constructively
- Session flow is seamless; transitions between segments are smooth and purposeful
- Uses wait time and silence effectively; does not rush to fill every pause

**Score 3 — Proficient**
- Uses at least three instructional strategies
- Session energy is generally maintained; trainer notices and responds when it drops
- Manages dominant participants; quieter participants are acknowledged
- Transitions are clear; agenda is visible and tracked throughout
- Good use of questioning; open questions predominate in discussion segments

**Score 2 — Developing**
- Primarily uses one or two strategies (typically lecture + Q&A)
- Session energy fluctuates without the trainer adapting
- Group dynamics management is reactive rather than proactive
- Some transitions are abrupt or poorly signposted
- Questions are often closed; discussion opportunities are missed

**Score 1 — Insufficient**
- Reads from materials throughout; no interaction strategy
- Session energy is flat from beginning; no response to participant disengagement
- Dominant participants monopolise discussion; quieter participants are not acknowledged
- No evidence of planned structure or time management

### Common Facilitation Mistakes (Coach Participants to Avoid)

- "Any questions?" as the primary engagement strategy — replace with targeted questioning
- Waiting for volunteers — use names and directed questions to distribute participation
- Talking over participants' answers — wait for completion before adding commentary
- Telling participants they are "wrong" — redirect with "That's one view; what do others think?"
- Under-using the sandbox — participants learn IMS by doing, not watching

## Competency 3: Assessment Administration

**Definition**: The trainer correctly sets up, invigilates, marks, and reports on Nexara MCQ assessments. They communicate assessment outcomes clearly and manage resubmission pathways with empathy and precision.

### Observable Evidence Descriptors

**Score 4 — Exemplary**
- Configures the assessment with zero errors (timing, attempt limits, participant IDs)
- Delivers a pre-assessment briefing that is clear, calm, and removes participant anxiety
- Maintains invigilation protocol throughout; no integrity gaps
- Marks accurately and communicates results with sensitivity
- Proactively explains the resubmission pathway to all participants at the outset
- Issues certificates same-session for pass participants

**Score 3 — Proficient**
- Assessment configuration is correct
- Pre-assessment briefing covers all required elements
- Invigilation is maintained for the full assessment period
- Marking is accurate; results communicated clearly
- Resubmission pathway explained for participants who need it

**Score 2 — Developing**
- Minor configuration errors (e.g. wrong time limit); corrected before impact
- Pre-assessment briefing is incomplete (missing one required element)
- Brief lapses in invigilation (e.g. leaving the room)
- One marking error identified; corrected when pointed out
- Resubmission pathway not clearly communicated

**Score 1 — Insufficient**
- Assessment not configured correctly; requires Nexara support intervention
- No pre-assessment briefing
- Invigilation protocol not followed
- Marking errors that affect pass/fail outcomes
- Unable to initiate certificate issuance

## Competency 4: Programme Management

**Definition**: The trainer manages the administrative and logistical lifecycle of training programmes — scheduling, enrolment, reporting, and materials version control — without requiring ongoing Nexara support.

### Observable Evidence Descriptors

This competency is primarily assessed via the **written assessment** (Module 5 questions) and the **programme management simulation exercise** in Day 2. It is observed in the delivery assessment only to the extent that the trainer demonstrates prepared, organised delivery.

**Score 4 — Exemplary**
- Pre-session admin is complete and accurate (participant list, materials, sandbox access, assessment bank loaded)
- Manages unexpected logistical issues (missing participant, AV failure, late start) calmly and without disrupting the session
- Demonstrates knowledge of version control requirements and can describe what triggers a retro-training obligation
- Knows when to escalate to Nexara and when to resolve locally

**Score 3 — Proficient**
- Pre-session admin is complete; minor gaps addressed quickly
- Handles common logistical issues without disruption
- Understands the version update process and reporting obligations

**Score 2 — Developing**
- Pre-session admin has gaps that require participant-visible recovery
- Logistical issues cause session delays
- Unclear on version control; relies on Nexara for routine update questions

**Score 1 — Insufficient**
- Significant pre-session preparation failures
- Unable to manage routine logistical issues without Nexara intervention

## Competency 5: Professionalism

**Definition**: The trainer presents to the standard expected of a Nexara representative — appropriate personal presentation, professional communication style, ethical conduct, and alignment with Nexara's values and co-branding requirements.

### Observable Evidence Descriptors

**Score 4 — Exemplary**
- Presentation is impeccable; materials are co-branded correctly and without errors
- Communication style is inclusive, respectful, and engaging throughout
- Demonstrates empathy in challenging situations (participant failure, group conflict)
- Actively promotes the value of the training to participants beyond the compliance requirement
- Manages time with precision; session starts and ends on schedule

**Score 3 — Proficient**
- Presentation is professional; materials meet co-branding standards
- Communication is clear and respectful
- Handles challenging situations constructively
- Time management is good; minor overruns are self-corrected

**Score 2 — Developing**
- Presentation is acceptable; one or two minor co-branding deviations noted
- Communication is generally professional but inconsistent in tone
- Some difficulty managing challenging situations
- Time management requires improvement; session overruns or segments are rushed

**Score 1 — Insufficient**
- Presentation is below professional standard
- Co-branding violations that breach Nexara IP requirements
- Communication style that excludes or alienates participants
- Significant time management failure affecting delivery completeness

## Competency Framework — Annual Review

Certified Internal Trainers are subject to an annual renewal process. Nexara reserves the right to require re-demonstration of competency if:

- A participant complaint is received relating to delivery quality
- A co-branding or IP violation is reported
- The trainer's renewal 4-hour refresher assessment scores below 75%
- The trainer has not delivered a cohort in more than 12 months

In these cases, Nexara L&D will schedule a targeted competency review before the trainer is permitted to deliver further cohorts.
