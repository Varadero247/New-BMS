# Train-the-Trainer — Programme Overview

**Programme**: Nexara IMS Train-the-Trainer (T3)
**Version**: 1.0
**Managed by**: Nexara Learning & Development

---

## 1. Purpose

The Train-the-Trainer programme extends Nexara's training portfolio by enabling client organisations to build internal delivery capacity. Rather than relying on Nexara Master Trainers for every cohort, a client designates up to eight individuals per organisation as Internal Certified Trainers. Once certified, these trainers can deliver the End User Training programme and any of the five Module Owner Training programmes to as many internal cohorts as needed within the licence scope and the current materials version.

This programme is the **capstone** of the Nexara training portfolio. It assumes participants already understand the IMS platform from an operational perspective and are now learning to teach others to use it effectively.

---

## 2. Target Audience

| Participant Profile | Rationale |
|---------------------|-----------|
| L&D coordinators or training managers | Primary trainers — will lead future cohorts |
| Senior compliance officers | Subject-matter experts who can contextualise content |
| Quality or HSE managers with training responsibilities | Module Owner Training subject experts |
| HR business partners with L&D remit | Suitable for End User and HR/Payroll module delivery |

**Prerequisite**: Participants should have completed at least one prior Nexara programme (Administrator, Module Owner, or End User) or have documented experience using the Nexara IMS platform in an operational role for at least three months.

**Not suitable for**: Participants with no prior Nexara platform exposure; junior staff without prior training delivery experience; external consultants not engaged under a Nexara Training Partner agreement.

## 3. Programme Structure

| Day | Theme | CPD Hours |
|-----|-------|-----------|
| Day 1 | Foundations — Adult Learning, Facilitation Skills, Nexara Curriculum | 7 |
| Day 2 | Delivery Certification — Assessment Delivery, Programme Management, Assessed Delivery | 7 |
| **Total** | | **14** |

Each day runs **08:30–17:00** with two 15-minute breaks and a 45-minute lunch. The maximum cohort size is **8 participants**, which is driven by the requirement for a 20-minute observed delivery slot per person on Day 2.

---

## 4. What Participants Learn

By the end of the programme, participants will be able to:

- Apply adult learning principles to compliance training design and delivery
- Facilitate interactive sessions using questioning, group discussion, and experiential learning
- Navigate and contextualise all Nexara source materials (End User and Module Owner programmes)
- Configure and invigilate the Nexara MCQ assessment system
- Manage a training programme lifecycle: scheduling, enrolment, reporting, version updates
- Deliver a structured 20-minute training segment to observable professional standard
- Comply with Nexara's co-branding, licensing, and materials version control requirements

## 6. Prerequisites and Readiness Checks

Before the cohort begins, Nexara L&D will:

1. **Participant suitability review** — confirm each participant meets the profile above (email submission)
2. **Platform access** — issue sandbox credentials to all participants (≥5 business days before Day 1)
3. **Pre-read** — participants required to complete the module overview in `00-programme-overview/PROGRAMME-OVERVIEW.md` and the End User self-paced module (if not already completed)
4. **Technology check** (virtual only) — 30-minute IT test call scheduled 2 business days before Day 1

## 8. Co-Branding Rules

Client organisations may co-brand materials delivered to their internal cohorts under the following rules:

- Client logo may be placed in the **co-branding zone** defined in `03-trainer-certification/CO-BRANDING-GUIDE.md` on slide templates and printed handouts
- The **Nexara name and logo** must appear on all materials; it may not be removed, reduced below the minimum size, or obscured
- The programme title must include the words "Nexara IMS" — e.g. "XYZ Corp Training Academy | Powered by Nexara IMS" is acceptable; "XYZ Corp Compliance Training" (Nexara removed) is not
- Client organisations may **not** white-label or rebrand content as their own proprietary programme
- Any client-added content (additional case studies, organisation-specific scenarios) must be clearly demarcated from Nexara source material

Violations of co-branding rules may result in suspension of the training licence.

---

## 9. Licensing Scope

The "Nexara Certified Internal Trainer" credential authorises the holder to:

- Deliver End User Training and Module Owner Training programmes to employees of the **employing organisation only**
- Use Nexara source materials and assessment banks for those internal cohorts
- Issue the "Nexara Certified [Programme] Participant" certificate to successful participants (generated via the Training Portal)

The credential does **not** authorise:

- Delivery to external clients, partner organisations, or subsidiaries (separate licence required)
- Modifications to the assessment question banks
- Self-certification of the T3 programme itself (Nexara Master Trainer required)
- Use of materials beyond the current licensed version (see `04-curriculum-handover/VERSION-CONTROL-GUIDE.md`)
