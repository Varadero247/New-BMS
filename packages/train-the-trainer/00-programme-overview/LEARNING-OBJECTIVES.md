# Train-the-Trainer — Learning Objectives

**Programme**: Nexara IMS Train-the-Trainer (T3)
**Bloom's Level**: Application (Day 1) → Synthesis and Evaluation (Day 2)

---

## Overview

Learning objectives are written at **Bloom's Taxonomy** levels appropriate to each day. Day 1 objectives operate at the **Apply** and **Analyse** levels — participants work with existing frameworks and techniques. Day 2 objectives operate at the **Evaluate** and **Create** levels — participants produce assessed outputs and demonstrate independent judgement.

---

## Programme-Level Outcomes

By the end of the two-day programme, successful participants will be able to:

1. **Design** effective training sessions for End User and Module Owner cohorts by applying adult learning principles to Nexara content
2. **Facilitate** interactive, engaging training sessions using appropriate questioning techniques, energy management, and group management strategies
3. **Deliver** a structured training segment to an observable professional standard, as evidenced by passing the observed delivery assessment at ≥ 70%
4. **Administer** Nexara MCQ assessments — including invigilation, marking, and certificate issuance — in compliance with Nexara assessment integrity standards
5. **Manage** the complete programme lifecycle: scheduling cohorts, maintaining version-controlled materials, submitting completion data to Nexara
6. **Operate** within Nexara's co-branding, intellectual property, and licensing framework without supervision

## Day 1 — Foundations

### Module 1: Adult Learning Theory & Compliance Training Psychology

| # | Objective | Bloom's Level |
|---|-----------|--------------|
| 1.1 | Explain the four principles of andragogy (Knowles) and apply each to the design of a compliance training session | Apply |
| 1.2 | Map Nexara module content to the appropriate levels of Bloom's Taxonomy and select corresponding instructional strategies | Analyse |
| 1.3 | Identify the three primary learning styles (VARK) and describe how to accommodate each within a single session | Apply |
| 1.4 | Explain the three stages of cognitive load theory and restructure a content-heavy slide deck to reduce extraneous load | Analyse |
| 1.5 | Describe the compliance training psychology effect ("box-ticking") and propose facilitation strategies that build genuine engagement | Apply |

### Module 2: Facilitation Skills

| # | Objective | Bloom's Level |
|---|-----------|--------------|
| 2.1 | Apply a minimum of four questioning techniques (open, probing, hypothetical, rhetorical) in a simulated training scenario | Apply |
| 2.2 | Demonstrate effective room management for groups of 8–16 participants, including seating configurations and group split strategies | Apply |
| 2.3 | Identify at least three "difficult participant" archetypes and select an appropriate de-escalation or re-engagement strategy for each | Analyse |
| 2.4 | Apply energy management techniques (pace variation, activity switching, physical movement) at appropriate points in a 90-minute session | Apply |
| 2.5 | Construct a session opening sequence that establishes psychological safety, sets expectations, and activates prior knowledge | Apply |

### Module 3: The Nexara Curriculum

| # | Objective | Bloom's Level |
|---|-----------|--------------|
| 3.1 | Navigate the complete file structure of the End User Training and all five Module Owner Training source packages | Apply |
| 3.2 | Identify which Nexara module content may and may not be customised, with reference to the Customisation Guide | Analyse |
| 3.3 | Set up and operate the Nexara IMS sandbox environment to support training delivery for any programme module | Apply |
| 3.4 | Explain the co-branding rules and identify at least three prohibited modifications | Apply |
| 3.5 | Locate and correctly use facilitator answer keys, distinguishing between participant-facing and facilitator-only materials | Apply |

### Lab — Day 1: Micro-Teaching Practice

| # | Objective | Bloom's Level |
|---|-----------|--------------|
| L1.1 | Deliver a 10-minute training segment from the Nexara curriculum using at least two facilitation techniques introduced in Module 2 | Apply |
| L1.2 | Receive structured peer feedback using the Day 1 feedback rubric and identify two areas for development | Analyse |
| L1.3 | Provide structured peer feedback to at least two other participants, using specific observable evidence | Evaluate |

## Day 2 — Delivery Certification

### Module 4: Assessment Delivery

| # | Objective | Bloom's Level |
|---|-----------|--------------|
| 4.1 | Configure and launch a Nexara MCQ assessment on the Training Portal, including time limits, participant IDs, and attempt settings | Apply |
| 4.2 | Describe the invigilation requirements for Nexara assessments (in-person and virtual) and identify three integrity breach scenarios | Analyse |
| 4.3 | Mark a completed assessment paper and calculate the percentage score correctly for pass/fail/distinction grading | Apply |
| 4.4 | Initiate the certificate issuance workflow on the Training Portal for a successful participant | Apply |
| 4.5 | Manage a failed assessment outcome — communicate the result, explain the resubmission pathway, and schedule the resubmission | Apply |

### Module 5: Programme Management

| # | Objective | Bloom's Level |
|---|-----------|--------------|
| 5.1 | Build a 12-month training calendar for a cohort of 50 end users and 12 module owners, allocating sessions within capacity limits | Create |
| 5.2 | Process a participant enrolment in the Training Portal, including role assignment and pre-programme communication | Apply |
| 5.3 | Generate and interpret a programme completion report from the Training Portal, identifying at-risk participants | Analyse |
| 5.4 | Apply the Nexara version update protocol when new materials are released — identify which sessions require retro-training | Apply |
| 5.5 | Submit the required post-programme data to Nexara L&D and interpret the Nexara quality audit report | Apply |
