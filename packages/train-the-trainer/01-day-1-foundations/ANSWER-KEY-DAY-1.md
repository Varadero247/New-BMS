## Formative Check Questions and Answers

**Q1.** According to Knowles' andragogy model, which principle explains why compliance training should begin by establishing relevance to the participant's role?

**Answer: A — Adults need to know why they are learning something before they will engage with it.**

*Explanation*: The "need to know" principle (Principle 1) holds that adults will not invest effort in learning until they understand why it is relevant to them. This is the theoretical basis for opening every Nexara session with a role-relevant hook rather than a feature list.

---

**Q2.** A training designer creates a 45-minute session that requires participants to fill in a form in the sandbox based on a realistic scenario. At which Bloom's Taxonomy level is this assessment activity primarily operating?

**Answer: C — Apply.**

*Explanation*: Filling in a form based on a scenario requires the participant to *use* their knowledge in a new context — the definition of Bloom's Level 3 (Apply). Remember is Level 1 (recall facts); Understand is Level 2 (explain); Analyse is Level 4 (compare, differentiate).

---

**Q3.** A participant tells you: "I learn best by doing, not by reading documents." Which VARK mode is this participant most likely describing?

**Answer: D — Kinaesthetic.**

*Explanation*: The Kinaesthetic mode (K in VARK) describes a preference for hands-on, experiential learning — doing, practising, and using concrete examples. Reading/Writing (R) prefers text; Visual (V) prefers diagrams; Auditory (A) prefers spoken explanation.

---

**Q4.** Which of the following is an example of *extraneous* cognitive load in a Nexara training session?

**Answer: B — Using database column names (e.g. `date_occurred`) instead of the UI labels shown to users (e.g. "Date of Occurrence").**

*Explanation*: Extraneous load is complexity added by the instructor that does not serve the learning objective. Using technical database terms that participants never see in the UI adds confusion without benefit. Intrinsic load (Option A) comes from the content itself; germane load (Option C) is the productive effort of learning.

---

**Q5.** The "box-ticking effect" in compliance training describes which of the following?

**Answer: A — Participants who attend and complete mandatory training without genuine engagement or intent to apply learning.**

*Explanation*: The box-ticking effect occurs when participants perceive compliance training as a bureaucratic requirement rather than a genuine learning opportunity, leading to low engagement, low retention, and low transfer to practice.

---

**Q6.** You are delivering a session and ask: "If you discovered on Day 2 of your cohort that your sandbox access was not working — what would you do?" What type of question is this?

**Answer: C — Hypothetical.**

*Explanation*: A hypothetical question presents a scenario and asks participants to reason through a response. It is designed to apply knowledge to a plausible future situation. Open questions (A) invite general response; probing questions (B) follow up on a previous answer; rhetorical questions (D) make a point without expecting a response.

---

**Q7.** A participant is consistently answering every question before others can respond. Which strategy is MOST effective for managing this in the group?

**Answer: B — Directed questioning: ask a specific question to another participant by name before the dominant participant can respond.**

*Explanation*: Directed questioning is the most effective and least disruptive technique. It re-distributes participation naturally without creating an awkward public correction. Silence (A) may not be enough; public redirection (C) embarrasses the participant and the group; ignoring (D) allows the pattern to continue.

---

**Q8.** According to the module content, which energy management technique is most effective when a training room's energy has dropped significantly after lunch?

**Answer: D — Immediate kinaesthetic activity: a short task requiring physical movement or hands-on action in the sandbox.**

*Explanation*: Post-lunch energy dips respond best to physical re-engagement. A lecture or Q&A session (options A and B) will not reliably recover a crashed room. A break (option C) was just taken. Kinaesthetic activity is the reliable recovery mechanism.

---

**Q9.** In the Nexara Module Owner Training package, which of the following files is for FACILITATOR USE ONLY and must never be shared with participants?

**Answer: A — `ANSWER-KEY-DAY-A.md`.**

*Explanation*: Any file with "ANSWER-KEY" in its name is facilitator-only. `ASSESSMENT-DAY-A.md` (B) is participant-facing. `MODULE-QUALITY-WORKFLOWS.md` (C) is participant-facing content. `LAB-DAY-A.md` (D) is shared — the scenario is given to participants; the expected-outcome section is trainer-only.

---

**Q10.** According to Nexara's customisation policy, which of the following modifications to source materials is explicitly permitted?

**Answer: C — Replacing the fictional scenario company name (e.g. "Acme Manufacturing") with the client organisation's name in lab scenarios.**

*Explanation*: Scenario contextualisation is explicitly permitted. Changing MCQ correct answers (A) is prohibited (assessment integrity). Removing the Nexara logo from materials (B) is prohibited (co-branding rules). Translating materials without Nexara approval (D) is prohibited.

## Notes for the Trainer

These questions are diagnostic, not summative. If multiple participants answer Q4 (cognitive load) or Q9 (facilitator-only files) incorrectly, revisit those points in Day 2 Module 4 (Assessment Administration) — both have direct implications for delivery practice.

If a participant scores 6 or below on self-assessment, check in with them privately before Day 2 begins. Their written assessment performance may be at risk.
