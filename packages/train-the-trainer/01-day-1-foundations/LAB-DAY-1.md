# Lab — Day 1: Micro-Teaching Practice

**Programme**: Nexara IMS Train-the-Trainer — Day 1
**Delivery time**: 14:30–15:45 (75 minutes)
**Lab type**: Formative practice (not assessed; peer feedback only)
**Maximum participants**: 8

---

## Scenario Backstory

You are each a newly onboarded internal trainer at Nexara's client, **Meridian Industrial Group** — a mid-size engineering and manufacturing company that has recently gone live with the Nexara IMS platform. Today is your first "practice cohort" — a small internal pilot before you run your first formal End User or Module Owner session.

Meridian has a mixed workforce: experienced engineers who are sceptical of new systems, newer operations staff who are comfortable with technology, and a handful of senior compliance officers who see the platform as an opportunity. Your cohort of practice participants (your fellow T3 delegates) represents this range.

Your objective: deliver a **10-minute segment** from the Nexara curriculum. Your specific segment has been assigned to you by the Nexara Master Trainer during the lunch break. Do not try to cover the full topic — cover your assigned segment with depth and engagement.

## Lab Steps

### Step 1 — Segment Assignment (during lunch, 12:15–13:00)

The Master Trainer assigns one segment to each participant and posts the delivery schedule on the flipchart:

| Delivery Order | Participant Name | Segment | Observer 1 | Observer 2 |
|---------------|-----------------|---------|-----------|-----------|
| 1 | | | | |
| 2 | | | | |
| (etc.) | | | | |

Each participant has approximately 15 minutes during Module 3 and the afternoon break to review their segment and plan their delivery.

---

### Step 2 — Delivery Preparation (13:00–14:30)

During Module 3, participants navigate to their assigned source file and familiarise themselves with the content. The trainer explicitly permits referencing the source material during delivery — this is not a memorisation exercise. Participants should identify:

- The **two or three key points** they will cover in 10 minutes
- **One question** they will ask the group (open, probing, or hypothetical)
- **One example** they will use (from the source material or contextualised to Meridian's industry)
- **One sandbox action** they will invite the group to complete (if sandbox is available)

---

### Step 3 — Room Setup (14:20–14:30)

Convert to cabaret seating if not already in this configuration. Each participant receives two peer feedback forms (one for each delivery they will observe). The trainer prepares the lab delivery schedule on the display.

---

### Step 4 — Delivery Sequence (14:30–15:40)

Each delivery slot:

| Time | Activity |
|------|---------|
| 0:00 | Trainer announces the participant and their topic |
| 0:00–10:00 | Participant delivers their segment (strictly timed; trainer holds timer) |
| 10:00 | Trainer calls "time" — participant stops mid-sentence if necessary (this is realistic) |
| 10:00–12:30 | Trainer gives one sentence of group-level observation (not individual critique) |
| 12:30–15:00 | Two assigned observers complete peer feedback forms (in writing; 2.5 minutes) |

*With 8 participants at ~15 min per slot: 8 × 15 = 120 min. Start at 14:30; all slots complete by approximately 16:30. Adjust to 9-min slots if needed to complete within the scheduled window.*

**Note**: The trainer observes all deliveries using the observer checklist but does **not** give individual feedback during the lab — this is practice, not assessment. Individual coaching occurs at the 15:45 debrief.

---

### Step 5 — Peer Feedback Form (completed by observers)

**Peer Feedback: Day 1 Micro-Teaching**

Delivery by: _____________________ | Topic: _____________________

Observer: _____________________

| Dimension | What I Observed |
|-----------|----------------|
| **Opening** — Did the delivery start with a clear, engaging hook? | |
| **Clarity** — Was the content explained clearly, without jargon? | |
| **Questions** — Did the trainer ask at least one question that invited participant response? | |
| **Pacing** — Was the delivery appropriately paced? Too fast / too slow / about right? | |
| **What worked best**: | |
| **One thing to try differently**: | |

*Instruction to observers: Be specific. "Good energy" is less useful than "You used an open question at the 4-minute mark that generated a genuine discussion." Use observations, not judgements.*

---

### Step 6 — Group Debrief (15:45–16:15)

At 15:45, the trainer opens the group debrief:

**Debrief Question 1**: "What did the best deliveries have in common? Give me observable behaviours — not 'they were good' but *what did they do*?"

**Debrief Question 2**: "What was the most common pattern you noticed across all deliveries — something almost everyone did or didn't do?"

**Debrief Question 3**: "If you could change one thing about your own delivery with the benefit of what you've seen today, what would it be?"

*Trainer: facilitate this discussion, do not present. Write emerging themes on the flipchart.*

---

### Step 7 — Individual Feedback Return (16:15–16:30)

Return the written peer feedback forms to each participant. Give participants 5 minutes to read them silently. Ask: "Is there anything on your feedback that surprised you?" Take two or three responses; close with the transition to the Day 1 formative check.

---

## Expected Outcomes

Successful participants will:
- Deliver a recognisable, structured 10-minute training segment
- Ask at least one question during their delivery
- Demonstrate awareness of their audience (maintain eye contact; adjust pace in response to responses)
- Accept peer feedback constructively

Common first-delivery patterns to note (not penalise):
- Reading directly from the source material for extended periods
- Forgetting to ask a question (telling instead of facilitating)
- Running over or under time significantly
- Failing to connect content to participant role context

These patterns are the reason the lab exists — they are expected in Day 1 practice and are the exact areas the Day 2 delivery assessment rewards improvement on.

---

## Extension Task (for participants who complete early)

For participants who finished early or whose delivery was notably strong:

> "Your End User Training session has 12 registered participants. Three of them have pre-emailed to say they already know the system — they've been using it in another role. How would you structure the session to accommodate them without boring them or alienating the other nine?"

*This extension task requires synthesis (Bloom's Level 6) and is designed to stretch the highest performers without slowing the lab for others.*

---

## Trainer Preparation Notes

- Assign segments that stretch each participant slightly beyond their comfort zone — a Quality manager should not be given the Quality NC segment; assign them the HSE or HR segment instead
- Pair observers who are likely to give genuinely different feedback — avoid pairing close colleagues
- The 10-minute time limit is non-negotiable in the Day 1 lab — enforcing it creates realistic conditions for Day 2's 20-minute limit
- Note which participants demonstrate energy, eye contact, and question use — these are the Day 2 front-runners
