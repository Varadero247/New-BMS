## Session Detail

### 08:30 — Welcome, Introductions, Day Objectives (30 min)

**Purpose**: Establish psychological safety; set expectations; activate prior knowledge.

**Energy note**: This is the most important 30 minutes of the programme. Participants arrive as experienced professionals — treat them as peers, not students. The tone set here determines whether they engage or coast.

**Sequence**:
1. **Trainer welcome** (3 min) — Brief Nexara L&D context; why T3 exists; what happens at the end if they succeed
2. **Participant introductions round** (15 min) — Each person: name, role, organisation, one word to describe their current confidence about training delivery
3. **Programme overview** (7 min) — Display agenda; confirm both assessment components; confirm Day 2 observed delivery slots
4. **Psychological contract** (5 min) — "What we need from each other": trainer commitment, participant commitment; address the "assessment anxiety" early

**Transition cue**: "Now let's start with why adults learn differently from children — and why that matters every time you stand up to deliver."

---

### 09:00 — Module 1: Adult Learning Theory & Compliance Training Psychology (90 min)

**Materials**: `MODULE-ADULT-LEARNING.md`

**Segment breakdown**:
- 09:00–09:20 — Andragogy principles (Knowles' four principles; applied to compliance context)
- 09:20–09:40 — Bloom's Taxonomy (cognitive levels; mapping to Nexara module objectives)
- 09:40–09:55 — Learning styles (VARK; practical accommodations in one session)
- 09:55–10:10 — Cognitive load (three types; designing IMS training that doesn't overload)
- 10:10–10:25 — Compliance training psychology (box-ticking effect; intrinsic vs. extrinsic motivation; strategies)
- 10:25–10:30 — Module 1 discussion prompt: "Which principle surprised you most? Why?"

**Energy note**: Module 1 is content-heavy. The trainer should NOT lecture for 90 minutes. Build in micro-activities: pair discussions at the 20-minute and 50-minute marks. Use the "one-word reaction" prompt after each section.

**Q&A handling**: Take questions in batches (after each 20-min segment); park complex questions in the "Parking Lot" flipchart. Revisit at the debrief.

**Common participant mistake to address proactively**: Confusing "learning style preferences" with "learning style categorisation being deterministic." Emphasise that accommodating multiple modalities is best practice; not every person needs a unique pathway.

---

### 10:30 — Morning Break (15 min)

**Trainer task during break**: Confirm participant name cards are visible; prepare Module 2 activity materials; check sandbox access is working for Module 3 (log in with trainer credentials to confirm).

---

### 10:45 — Module 2: Facilitation Skills (90 min)

**Materials**: `MODULE-FACILITATION-SKILLS.md`

**Segment breakdown**:
- 10:45–11:00 — Room management (layout options; furniture reconfiguration for labs; managing breakout spaces)
- 11:00–11:25 — Questioning techniques (open, probing, hypothetical, rhetorical; live demonstration with participants as responders)
- 11:25–11:45 — Managing difficult participants (four archetypes: the dominator, the disengaged, the sceptic, the over-eager; role-play)
- 11:45–12:00 — Energy management (pace variation; physical movement; activity switching; reading the room)
- 12:00–12:15 — Putting it together: opening a session (sample 5-minute opening; participants practise the first 60 seconds of an opening in pairs)

**Demonstration note**: The trainer should use the participants themselves as subjects for questioning demonstrations. Ask a question; receive the answer; then deconstruct: "What kind of question was that? What did it achieve?"

**Energy note**: By 11:30, energy typically dips post-coffee. Schedule the role-play (difficult participants) here — it is the most engaging segment and reliably lifts energy.

**Transition cue**: "You now have a vocabulary for facilitation. This afternoon you'll apply it. But first, let's look at what you'll be facilitating — the Nexara curriculum itself."

---

### 12:15 — Lunch (45 min)

**Trainer task during lunch**: Assign micro-teaching segments to each participant (see LAB-DAY-1.md for segment menu). Write names and segments on the lab schedule poster. Ensure participants know their segment before starting Module 3.

---

### 13:00 — Module 3: The Nexara Curriculum (75 min)

**Materials**: `MODULE-NEXARA-CURRICULUM.md`

**Segment breakdown**:
- 13:00–13:20 — File structure walkthrough (navigating End User + Module Owner packages; finding facilitator vs. participant materials)
- 13:20–13:40 — Lab logistics and sandbox setup (live sandbox demo; common setup issues; sample data overview)
- 13:40–13:55 — Customisation limits (what may be edited; what is prohibited; worked example: "Can I replace the scenario company name?")
- 13:55–14:05 — Branding rules (co-branding zone; prohibited modifications; the "Nexara must always appear" rule)
- 14:05–14:15 — Open Q&A on curriculum materials

**Participant activity**: During the file structure walkthrough, participants work with their own laptops/tablets and navigate the source packages themselves. This is not a presentation — it is guided exploration.

**Energy note**: Module 3 is naturally interactive because participants are hands-on with their materials. Do not turn it into a lecture. Ask: "Who can find the answer key file for Day A Quality?" rather than showing the path.

**Transition cue**: "You have 15 minutes now — when you come back, you'll deliver. You've been assigned your segment. Read it, don't memorise it. Be ready to teach, not recite."

---

### 14:15 — Afternoon Break (15 min)

**Trainer task during break**: Finalise the lab delivery order. Post the schedule on the room display. Set up peer feedback forms at each seat.

---

### 14:30 — Lab: Micro-Teaching Practice (75 min)

**Materials**: `LAB-DAY-1.md`

**Format**: Each participant delivers a **10-minute segment** from the Nexara curriculum. Two other participants observe and give structured peer feedback using the Day 1 feedback form. Trainer observes all deliveries but does not interrupt.

**With 8 participants**: 8 × 10 min = 80 min (runs slightly over; trim to 9-min slots if needed or start break 5 min early).

**Energy note**: This is the session highlight. Energy is high when delivery starts; may drop around participant 5–6. Recharge: brief "lightning round" discussion between deliveries — "What one word describes your reaction to that delivery?"

**Trainer role**: Silent observer during delivery; takes notes on the observer checklist. Delivers brief (60-second) group feedback between slots — save individual feedback for the debrief.

**Transition cue**: "Good work. Every one of you showed something valuable. Now let's unpack what we saw."

---

### 15:45 — Debrief + Curriculum Q&A (45 min)

**Sequence**:
1. **Group reflection** (10 min) — "What did the best deliveries have in common?"
2. **Trainer observations** (10 min) — Structured feedback on patterns observed across the cohort; no individual callouts by name
3. **Individual feedback distribution** (5 min) — Written peer feedback forms returned to each participant
4. **Curriculum Q&A** (15 min) — Open Q&A on modules, materials, labs, sandbox
5. **Parking lot review** (5 min) — Address all questions noted during the day

**Energy note**: Debrief energy is typically reflective and slightly tired. Do not let this become a lecture. Facilitate; don't present.

---

### 16:30 — Day 1 Formative Check + Day 2 Briefing (30 min)

**Formative check** (15 min): 10-question self-assessment (not submitted for marking; participants complete individually then compare with a partner). Questions cover Day 1 content. Answers on the back of the form. Purpose: identify gaps before Day 2.

**Day 2 briefing** (15 min):
- Confirm observed delivery slot times (publish the schedule)
- Explain the observer checklist and what the assessor looks for
- Confirm the written assessment timing and format
- Remind: Day 2 starts at 08:30 — no late arrivals for assessed delivery

**Closing**: "Tomorrow you become the trainer. We'll assess you the same way your future participants will judge you. Use tonight to practise your segment one more time — and to get a good night's sleep."
