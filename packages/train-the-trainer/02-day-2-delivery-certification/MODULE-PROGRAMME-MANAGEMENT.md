## Section 1: Scheduling Cohorts

Effective programme management begins with a realistic 12-month training calendar. Internal certified trainers are responsible for scheduling, organising, and running all End User and Module Owner cohorts within their organisation — without requiring Nexara operational support for day-to-day scheduling.

### Capacity Planning Framework

Before building the calendar, establish your constraints:

| Constraint | Typical Range | Your Organisation's Limit |
|------------|--------------|--------------------------|
| Maximum cohort size — End User Training | Up to 16 participants | |
| Maximum cohort size — Module Owner Training | Up to 14 participants | |
| Maximum cohorts per trainer per week | 2 (one trainer cannot sustainably deliver more) | |
| Minimum cohort size | 4 (below this, rescheduling is recommended) | |
| Room capacity (your training room) | [Your figure] | |
| Blackout periods (busy periods, audits, holidays) | Variable | |

**Worked example**: Meridian Industrial Group has 200 end users to train in a single quarter. Their training room holds 16. With one trainer delivering two cohorts per week, they can run 2 cohorts × 13 weeks = 26 sessions, covering 26 × 16 = 416 potential seats. Capacity is sufficient. The constraint is trainer availability — if the trainer is involved in other L&D activity, the schedule tightens.

### Building the 12-Month Calendar

**Step 1**: Map all participant groups — who needs End User training, who needs Module Owner training (which day), and by when.

**Step 2**: Identify blackout periods — peak production periods, annual audits, Christmas shutdown, school holiday periods (if relevant to your workforce).

**Step 3**: Allocate sessions from highest-priority groups first — regulatory deadline dates (e.g. certification audit in Q3 means all module owners trained by end of Q2).

**Step 4**: Build in buffer — do not fill every week. Leave at least 2 weeks per month unscheduled for make-up sessions and new-starter cohorts.

**Step 5**: Review with your HR/L&D manager before communicating to departments — departmental blackout periods (e.g. month-end close for Finance) are often invisible in the global calendar.

### Scheduling Workshop (in-session exercise)

You are the new internal trainer at Meridian. Your schedule for Q2 (April–June) must include:
- 50 end users across three departments (Finance: 12, Operations: 28, HR: 10)
- 6 module owners — Finance team need Finance & Contracts (Day D); Operations need HSE (Day B); HR need HR & Payroll (Day C)
- Maximum 2 cohorts per week; maximum room capacity 16
- Finance month-end: last 5 working days of each month are blackout for Finance staff
- Operations: shift rotation means Wednesday afternoon is available; all other afternoons are unavailable

*Build a Q2 schedule grid and present to the group. Be ready to explain your prioritisation decisions.*

## Section 2: Participant Enrolment

### Training Portal Enrolment Process

1. Navigate to **Training → Cohorts → Create New Cohort**
2. **Set cohort details**: programme name, date, trainer (yourself), location/URL
3. **Enrol participants**: Click **Add Participants** → search by name or email → add; or bulk-upload via CSV
4. **Set participant roles**: End User participants receive the "end-user" role; Module Owner participants receive the relevant "module-owner-[day]" role (this controls which sandbox modules they can access)
5. **Send pre-programme communication**: Click **Notify Cohort** — portal sends the standard pre-programme email (joining instructions, sandbox login, pre-read link)
6. **Confirm enrolment**: 48 hours before the session, run the enrolment report to confirm all participants have accepted / confirmed attendance

### Pre-Programme Communication (Standard Email)

The portal sends the following to enrolled participants:

> *Subject: Your Nexara Training — [Programme Name] on [Date]*
>
> *Dear [Name],*
>
> *You are enrolled in the [Programme Name] training session on [Date] at [Location/URL].*
>
> *Programme start time: [Time]. Please arrive/join 10 minutes early.*
>
> *Pre-programme preparation (required):*
> *1. Complete the Nexara End User self-paced module (link below) — takes approximately 30 minutes*
> *2. Log in to the Nexara sandbox to confirm your access (credentials below)*
> *3. Read the one-page programme overview (attached)*
>
> *Sandbox credentials: [Username] / [Password]*
> *Sandbox URL: [URL]*
>
> *Questions: [your email]*

**Customising the pre-programme email**: The email template is editable. You may add organisation-specific context (e.g. why the system is being implemented, who to contact with operational questions). You may not remove the Nexara branding or the Nexara sandbox access instructions.

### Managing Non-Enrolment

Participants who do not complete pre-programme steps (sandbox login not tested, pre-read not done) are more likely to struggle in the session. Strategies:

- **For large groups**: Set an automated reminder 5 days before the session (portal sends this if configured)
- **For line managers with high non-compliance**: Send a "manager copy" email 5 days before — line managers respond well to visibility
- **At the session start**: Don't penalise participants who didn't pre-read; catch them up efficiently ("If you haven't done the pre-read, that's fine — here's what you need to know")

## Section 3: Completion Reporting and Nexara Submission

### In-Portal Completion Reports

After each session, the Training Portal automatically generates:

| Report | Contents | Retention |
|--------|---------|---------|
| **Cohort completion report** | Participant names, attendance, assessment scores, pass/fail | Permanent (portal archive) |
| **Certificate register** | All certificates issued with certificate numbers and dates | Permanent |
| **Non-completions log** | Participants enrolled but not completed (absent, deferred, or failed) | Until completion or close |

Access all reports at **Training → Reports → [Cohort Name]**.

### Submitting Completion Data to Nexara

Within **5 business days** of each cohort completing, submit the cohort completion data to Nexara L&D:

1. Export the cohort completion report (PDF or CSV) from the Training Portal
2. Email to training@nexara.io with subject line: "Cohort Completion — [Organisation] — [Programme] — [Date]"
3. Include: total enrolled, total completed (pass), total incomplete (fail/absent), any notes on integrity incidents

**Why this matters**: Nexara uses this data to maintain the central trainer register, verify your annual renewal status, and audit training quality across its certified trainer network. Missing submissions are flagged; repeated non-submission can trigger a compliance review.

### Interpreting the Nexara Quality Audit Report

Twice per year, Nexara L&D sends each certified trainer a quality audit report covering their cohorts from the previous six months. Metrics included:

| Metric | Target | Interpretation |
|--------|--------|---------------|
| **Cohort completion rate** | > 85% | % of enrolled participants who complete the programme (attend + pass assessment) |
| **First-attempt pass rate** | > 75% | % of assessment attempts that pass on the first try |
| **Repeat attendance rate** | < 10% | % of participants who attend more than one session (excludes legitimate resubmissions) |
| **NPS (Net Promoter Score)** | > 50 | From post-session participant survey; average satisfaction |

**Low NPS (< 30)**: Indicates participant dissatisfaction with delivery quality; Nexara may contact you for a quality conversation.

**Low first-attempt pass rate (< 60%)**: Indicates participants are not adequately prepared before assessment; review your pre-programme communication and session content delivery.

## Section 4: Version Control — Nexara Material Updates

### The Nexara Update Cycle

Nexara releases material updates on a **quarterly cycle** (January, April, July, October). Updates may include:

- New or revised module content reflecting platform updates
- Updated assessment questions (all changes managed by Nexara; answer keys are updated simultaneously)
- New scenarios or labs
- Regulatory compliance updates (e.g. new legislation affecting the Legal Register module)

### Receiving an Update

When a Nexara material update is released, certified trainers receive an email from training@nexara.io containing:

- **New version number** (e.g. v1.0 → v1.1 or v1.0 → v2.0)
- **Change summary**: a plain-language description of what changed
- **Retro-training flag**: explicitly states whether existing certified participants require retro-training
- **Download link**: new versions of affected files
- **Implementation deadline**: date by which all new cohorts must use the new version

### Your Responsibilities on Receiving an Update

| Action | Timing |
|--------|--------|
| Download and review the updated files | Within 3 business days of receiving the email |
| Replace the old version in your materials folder | Before the implementation deadline |
| Schedule retro-training sessions (if flagged) | Within the window specified in the update email |
| Confirm to Nexara that you've implemented the update | Reply to the update email with "Confirmed — updated [date]" |
| Do NOT use old version materials after the implementation deadline | Non-compliance affects your trainer licence |

### Retro-Training Trigger Conditions

Retro-training is required when a content change affects **participant behaviour** in the system — what they must do differently going forward. Examples:

| Change Type | Retro-Training? | Rationale |
|-------------|----------------|-----------|
| New field added to the incident form (mandatory) | ✓ Yes | Participants must know to complete the new field |
| Assessment question reworded (same correct answer) | ✗ No | No behavioural change |
| New module added to the platform | ✓ Yes (for module owners of that module) | New functionality requires training |
| Terminology change (field renamed in UI) | ✓ Yes | Participants may become confused by UI mismatch |
| Background content updated (explanatory text only) | ✗ No | No behavioural change |

**When uncertain**: Email training@nexara.io. Always ask rather than assume.

## Section 5: Escalation to Nexara

### When to Escalate to Nexara

Internal certified trainers are empowered to resolve most training delivery issues independently. Escalate to Nexara (training@nexara.io) in the following situations:

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Sandbox is down and cannot be resolved | Email with subject "URGENT: Sandbox Outage — [Date] Cohort" |
| Participant disputes their assessment result | Do not re-mark yourself; escalate to Nexara for independent review |
| Suspected assessment integrity breach | Report within 24 hours; provide documentation |
| Request to deliver outside licensed organisation scope | Always seek approval before agreeing to any external delivery |
| Co-branding dispute or copyright query | Nexara legal team will respond within 5 business days |
| Participant requires accessibility accommodation | Nexara L&D manages accommodation requests (extended time, alternative format) |
| Trainer illness/unavailability for scheduled cohort | Notify Nexara as early as possible; Nexara may provide emergency master trainer cover |

### When NOT to Escalate (Resolve Locally)

| Situation | Local Resolution |
|-----------|----------------|
| Participant arrives late | Brief them at break; accommodate if <15 min; cannot admit if >30 min into session |
| AV/IT failure in training room | Use the contingency procedures in `05-delivery-logistics/IN-PERSON-DELIVERY-GUIDE.md` |
| Participant requests additional time for sandbox exercise | Permit within your session schedule; do not extend the assessment window |
| Participant challenges content accuracy | Confirm against source materials; if in doubt, say "I'll verify and confirm by email" |
| Department manager requests a date change after booking | Manage directly; re-schedule in the portal |

### Nexara Support SLA for Certified Trainers

| Query Type | Response Time |
|-----------|-------------|
| Urgent (sandbox outage, integrity breach) | Within 4 business hours |
| Standard (version update, scheduling query) | Within 2 business days |
| Administrative (completion submission, register query) | Within 5 business days |
