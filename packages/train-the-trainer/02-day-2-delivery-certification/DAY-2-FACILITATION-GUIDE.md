# Day 2 Facilitation Guide — Delivery Certification

**Programme**: Nexara IMS Train-the-Trainer (T3)
**Audience**: Nexara Master Trainer (this document is not distributed to participants)
**Purpose**: Full lead-trainer script for Day 2 with timing, assessment management, and debrief methodology

---

## 08:30 — Recap + Day 2 Overview (30 min)

### Opening (2 min)

*Walk to the front before participants settle. Stand; do not sit.*

**Script**:
> "Good morning. Day 2. Today you get assessed — and then you get certified, or you get a clear path to certification. Either way, today ends with clarity.
>
> Before we start: I reviewed your formative checks from yesterday. Most of you are in good shape. A few areas came up repeatedly — I want to address those now before we go into this morning's content."

---

### Formative Check Review (10 min)

*Address only the two or three areas where multiple participants showed uncertainty. Do not single out individuals.*

**Script template**:
> "Several people were uncertain on [cognitive load / facilitator-only files / resubmission policy]. Let me spend two minutes on that now because it's going to come up in the written assessment this afternoon.
>
> [Deliver targeted 90-second explanation. Take one question. Move on.]"

---

### Assessment Briefing (10 min)

**Script**:
> "Here's today's structure. [Display agenda.] Two modules this morning — assessment delivery and programme management. Both directly testable this afternoon.
>
> At 13:00 the observed deliveries begin. Your slot is [confirm slot times]. At 15:15 the written assessment starts. Both must pass for certification today.
>
> Let me show you the observer checklist." [Display `DELIVERY-OBSERVER-CHECKLIST.md` — participant can see the domains and scale but not the full descriptors.]
>
> "Five domains. 4-point scale. You need a 2.5 average overall, and no domain below a 2. You've seen that document before — it's in your pack. Study it in the break before your delivery slot.
>
> Questions before we start Module 4?"

*Pause and wait. Address any questions directly — do not defer assessment process questions to "later."*

**Transition**:
> "Right. Module 4 — assessment delivery. This is the shortest path to understanding what your participants will experience on the other side of this table."

---

## 09:00 — Module 4: Assessment Delivery (90 min)

*Refer to `MODULE-ASSESSMENT-DELIVERY.md` for full content. Use the following script for key demonstration moments.*

### Mock Assessment Demonstration (09:00–09:20)

**Script before demonstration**:
> "Before I explain how to run an assessment, I want you to experience being a participant in one. Five questions. I'll launch it now. You have 5 minutes. Complete it genuinely — don't look at your neighbour."

*Launch the mock assessment on the portal. Time it. At 5 minutes:*
> "Stop. Before I show you the admin view — what was that experience like? Two words. Go around the room."

*Listen to responses — common: "pressured," "uncertain," "clear," "fine," "anxious." Use these to open the discussion on pre-assessment participant briefing.*

> "You felt that. Your participants will feel that too — more so, because they haven't had two days of preparation. Everything you say in the three minutes before you say 'Begin' determines whether they perform at their capability or below it."

---

### Invigilation Protocol (09:35–09:55)

**Script**:
> "Invigilation sounds formal. It is formal. The reason: if you administer an assessment without proper invigilation and a participant cheats, the certificate you issue is invalid. That reflects on you, on Nexara, and on your organisation's compliance evidence.
>
> Three invigilation rules. One: no reference materials open during the assessment — not printed, not on screen, not as a browser tab. Two: no communication between participants during the assessment — verbal or written, deliberate or accidental. Three: you must be in the room, visible, for the full duration.
>
> What do you do if someone appears to be using notes? [Pause for responses.] You address it calmly and directly — 'Please close that document' — and note the time in writing. You do not accuse; you redirect. After the assessment, report to Nexara if you believe it affected the outcome."

---

### Managing Failed Outcomes (10:10–10:25)

**Script**:
> "This is the conversation nobody wants to have. But if you run assessments, you will have it. Here's how.
>
> First: never announce a fail publicly. Results are communicated 1:1. Always.
>
> Second: use this script." [Display / read the communication script from `MODULE-ASSESSMENT-DELIVERY.md` Section 5.]
>
> "The key is: clear, factual, solution-focused. 'You scored 70% — the pass threshold is 75%. That means you have five questions to get right next time. Here's how the resubmission works.' Not: 'I'm sorry, you didn't make it this time.' The apology makes it worse."

**Transition to break**:
> "Take 15 minutes. When you come back, we shift to the organisational view — how do you manage a whole training programme, not just a single session."

---

## 10:45 — Module 5: Programme Management (90 min)

*Refer to `MODULE-PROGRAMME-MANAGEMENT.md` for full content.*

### Scheduling Workshop (11:05–11:25)

**Setup**:
> "Work in pairs. You're the newly certified internal trainer at Meridian Industrial Group. 50 end users to train, 12 module owners across three departments. You have one available room (capacity: 16), and you can run a maximum of two cohorts per week. Build a 12-month plan. Go — you have 15 minutes."

*Circulate; observe; prompt pairs who are stuck:* "What's your constraint — is it room capacity or your own availability?" "What happens if someone misses their scheduled cohort?"

**Debrief (5 min)**:
> "What constraints did you find? What did you have to make decisions about?"
>
> *Expected responses*: conflicting department availability; back-to-back days being exhausting; module owner training requiring different segmentation from end user training.
>
> "Good. Those are real constraints. The version control section coming up adds one more: what do you do when Nexara releases an updated module and three of your scheduled cohorts are using the old version?"

---

### Version Control (11:45–12:05)

**Critical script**:
> "Nexara releases material updates on a quarterly cycle. When an update arrives, you receive an email with the version number, a change summary, and a retro-training flag — which means: does this change require you to go back to participants who completed training before this update?
>
> The rule: if the change affects compliance behaviour — what participants need to *do* or *record* differently — retro-training is required. If the change is editorial (wording, examples, layout), it is not.
>
> You don't decide this. The email tells you. But you are responsible for acting on it within the window specified."

## 13:00 — Assessed Delivery Block (120 min)

**Script before first delivery**:
> "We're in assessment mode now. I will not give feedback during or between deliveries — that happens in the 1:1 debriefs later. The rest of the group: your role is to be genuine participants. Ask a realistic question when one naturally arises. Don't throw curve balls — behave as your actual End User cohort would behave.
>
> [Name], you're first. Your segment is [topic]. You have 20 minutes from the moment you say your first word. Over to you."

*Step back. Sit. Score.*

### Trainer Scoring Protocol

For each delivery:
1. Note observable evidence against each competency domain — not impressions, evidence
2. Score each domain after the delivery (not during — you cannot listen and score simultaneously)
3. If delivery ends before 20 minutes: note the time; it is not automatically a failure, but it suggests time management issues
4. If delivery goes beyond 22 minutes: call time firmly: "I'm going to stop you there — time is up." Do not let it run.

**Between deliveries**: Quiet transition only. A brief pause for water; no comments. "Thank you — [Next name], you're up."

---

## 15:00 — Break + Scoring Completion (15 min)

This is the trainer's most cognitively intensive 15 minutes. Complete all scoring before the written assessment begins. If you cannot complete all scoring in 15 minutes, ask participants to extend break by 5 minutes — do not rush scores.

---

## 15:15 — Written Assessment (30 min)

**Invigilation script**:
> "You have 20 questions and 30 minutes. Closed book — no notes, no devices, no reference materials. I will call 'five minutes remaining.' When I call time, you stop — even mid-sentence.
>
> Any questions before we begin? [Pause.] Good. Begin now." [Note start time.]

*At 25 minutes*: "Five minutes remaining."
*At 30 minutes*: "Time's up. Please submit or put down your pen."

*Collect papers / confirm portal submissions. Briefly step out for 5 minutes to compose yourself before the 1:1 debriefs begin.*

---

## 15:45 — Individual Debrief (45 min)

**Debrief script for certified participants**:
> "Your delivery score is [X]% — you pass. Your strongest area was [domain] — specifically, I noticed [observable evidence]. The area you'll want to keep developing is [domain] — here's what I saw. Congratulations. Your certificate will be ready at 16:30."

**Debrief script for conditional participants** (delivery fail; written pass):
> "Your written result is [X]% — that's a pass; well done. Your delivery score today is [X]% — that's just below the pass threshold. Specifically, in [domain], I needed to see [specific behaviour] and I saw [what actually happened].
>
> This isn't over. You have a 30-day resubmission window. You'll receive an email tonight with the options. You'll deliver a different 20-minute segment — your written result is banked. You're close. One more delivery, and you'll have it."

**Debrief script for full retake participants**:
> "Your delivery score today is [X]% and your written result is [X]%. Neither is at the pass threshold. I want to be direct with you: you'll need to come back for the next T3 cohort.
>
> Before you think about that: here's specifically what you'll need to develop. On the delivery side: [key area]. On the written: you'll benefit from reviewing [Module 4/5]. The next cohort is [estimated date — check with training@nexara.io].
>
> This doesn't mean you won't be a great internal trainer — it means today wasn't the day. I've seen people come back stronger."

---

## 16:30 — Certificate Ceremony + Close (30 min)

**Certificate presentation script** (for each certified participant):
> "[Name], it's my pleasure to present you with the Nexara Certified Internal Trainer certificate — End User and Module Owner Programmes. [Hand certificate.] Well done."

*Take group photograph if the cohort is comfortable with this — good for their CPD records and for Nexara case study use (with consent).*

**Closing script**:
> "Two days ago you were here as learners. You leave today either as certified trainers or as trainers in progress. The curriculum packages are in your inbox — that's yours to use.
>
> One last thing. The day you stop learning from your cohorts is the day your delivery goes stale. Keep asking your participants what worked and what didn't. Bring that back to your programme design. That's what makes the difference between someone who delivers training and someone who trains.
>
> Thank you. It's been a privilege."
