**Bloom's level target**: Apply

## Section 1: Configuring the Training Portal Assessment

The Nexara Training Portal provides a built-in MCQ assessment system used for all End User and Module Owner assessments. As a certified internal trainer, you will use this system to create, launch, and score assessments for your cohorts.

### Assessment Configuration Steps

1. **Log in to the Training Portal** with your trainer credentials (`training.admin@[your-org]` role assigned by Nexara on certification)
2. Navigate to **Assessments → Manage Assessments → Create New**
3. **Select the assessment template**: Choose from the available Nexara-provided templates (End User Assessment, or the specific Module Owner day — A, B, C, D, or E)
4. **Set the cohort**: Link the assessment to the current cohort (participants must be enrolled before the assessment is launched)
5. **Configure settings**:
   - **Time limit**: 30 minutes (locked; cannot be adjusted by internal trainers)
   - **Attempt limit**: 1 per session (resubmission requires a new session created by Nexara L&D)
   - **Start mode**: Manual (you click "Begin" when the cohort is ready; do not use "Scheduled start" for in-person delivery)
   - **Randomisation**: Question order is randomised by default; do not disable
6. **Review participant list**: Confirm all enrolled participants are visible; add any late additions before launching
7. **Launch**: Click "Begin Assessment" — all enrolled participants receive simultaneous access

### Common Configuration Mistakes

| Mistake | Consequence | Prevention |
|---------|-------------|-----------|
| Launching before all participants are ready | Some participants have less than 30 minutes | Confirm all are logged in before clicking "Begin" |
| Using "Scheduled start" for in-person delivery | Assessment starts while you're still briefing | Always use manual start for synchronous delivery |
| Not confirming participant list before launch | Missing participants cannot join mid-assessment | Run participant check 10 minutes before launch |
| Disabling randomisation | Participants can share question order; integrity risk | Never disable randomisation |

## Section 2: The Pre-Assessment Participant Briefing

The 3 minutes before you say "Begin" are critical. A well-delivered pre-assessment briefing:

- Reduces anxiety (which improves performance)
- Ensures all participants understand the rules (which protects integrity)
- Sets the professional tone for the assessment experience

### Standard Briefing Script

> "We're about to begin the [End User / Module Owner — Day X] assessment. 20 questions. 30 minutes. Here are the rules:
>
> One: this is a closed-book assessment. No reference materials, no handouts, no browser tabs with training content, no mobile devices. If you have reference materials on your desk, please close them now. [Pause and observe.]
>
> Two: no communication with other participants during the assessment — no talking, no signalling, no passing anything.
>
> Three: if you finish early, please review your answers before submitting. Do not leave the room until time is called.
>
> If you have a technical issue during the assessment — your device freezes, the portal times out — raise your hand immediately and I will come to you. Do not try to fix it yourself.
>
> The pass threshold is 75% — 15 out of 20 correct. If you pass, you receive your certificate today. If you don't pass on the first attempt, the resubmission process gives you a path forward.
>
> Any questions before we begin? [Pause.] Good. I'm starting the timer now — begin."

### Adapting for Anxious Participants

If a participant is visibly anxious before the assessment:
- Speak to them privately for 30 seconds before the group briefing: "You know this. You've spent two days with this content. Trust yourself."
- Do not give additional study time — it creates unfairness
- If they request a bathroom break before the assessment: permit it; wait for their return before starting the timer

## Section 3: Invigilation Requirements

### In-Person Invigilation Protocol

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| **Presence** | Remain in the room for the full 30 minutes; do not step out even briefly |
| **Positioning** | Stand or sit at the back or side of the room — not at the front; you should be able to see all participants and their screens/papers |
| **Devices** | Your own device should be locked during assessment (no typing, no phone); your visible engagement with other tasks undermines the integrity message |
| **Silence** | Maintain complete silence during the assessment; do not answer content questions during assessment |
| **Time calls** | Call "5 minutes remaining" at the 25-minute mark; call "Time" at 30 minutes |
| **Early finishers** | If a participant submits early, they may review their answers on-screen but must remain seated and silent until time is called |

### Virtual Invigilation Protocol

For virtual delivery assessments via Zoom or Microsoft Teams:

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| **Camera** | All participants must have cameras on during the assessment |
| **Screen share** | Ask participants to share their screen (Training Portal assessment only — not their full desktop) |
| **Breakout rooms** | Do NOT use breakout rooms during assessment — all participants visible in main session |
| **Chat** | Disable participant-to-participant chat during assessment window |
| **Monitoring** | Watch gallery view; flag any participant whose camera goes off or who appears to be looking off-screen frequently |

### Integrity Breach Scenarios

| Scenario | Correct Response |
|----------|----------------|
| Participant is visibly using a reference document | Calmly and quietly: "Please close that document." Note time and nature of observation in writing. |
| Two participants appear to be communicating | Move physically between them (in-person) or break their connection verbally: "[Name], [Name] — please keep eyes on your own screen." Note observation. |
| Participant leaves the room mid-assessment | Pause the clock if possible; note the time out and time in; if unable to pause the portal timer, note the absence |
| Participant requests a content question to be answered | "I can't answer content questions during the assessment — trust your preparation." Do not answer. |
| Suspected deliberate breach | Complete the assessment; document fully; report to Nexara L&D before issuing any results |

**Important**: Do not accuse a participant during the assessment. Complete the session, document, and report. Nexara L&D will review and determine whether to invalidate results.

## Section 4: Marking and Results Communication

### Portal-Administered Assessments (Standard)

When using the Training Portal, marking is automatic — the system calculates the percentage score and displays pass/fail immediately on participant submission. Your role is:

1. **Confirm** each participant has submitted (check the admin view — pending submissions are highlighted)
2. **Export** the results report from **Assessments → Results → Export PDF** (store this; it is your programme record)
3. **Communicate results** 1:1 — never announce pass or fail publicly, even if all participants passed

### Paper-Administered Assessments (Contingency)

If the portal is unavailable and you use printed papers:

**Marking procedure**:
1. Load the answer key (`ANSWER-KEY-WRITTEN.md` or relevant `ANSWER-KEY-DAY-*.md`) on your device
2. Mark each paper against the answer key; record the score (number correct out of 20)
3. Calculate the percentage: score ÷ 20 × 100
4. Annotate the paper with score, pass/fail, and your initials and the date
5. Enter results into the Training Portal before end of day (offline results must be manually recorded)

**Handling ambiguous paper answers**: If a participant's handwritten answer is unclear (e.g. B/D ambiguous writing), award the mark if any reasonable interpretation matches the correct answer. Document ambiguous cases in your notes.

### Results Communication Script

**Pass result**:
> "Good news — you scored [X]%, which is above the 75% pass threshold. Your certificate will be issued today. Well done."

**Fail result** (1:1, private):
> "Your score today was [X]% — the pass threshold is 75%, so you need to return for the resubmission assessment. I know that's not the result you were hoping for. Here's specifically what to review: [identify the weakest topic area from the results]. The resubmission process is straightforward — you'll receive details by email."

## Section 5: Certificate Issuance

### Issuing via Training Portal

1. Navigate to **Assessments → Results → [Cohort Name]**
2. Select all participants with a PASS status
3. Click **Issue Certificates** — the portal generates individual certificates and sends them to the participant's registered email
4. Optionally: **Print** certificates for in-person ceremony (use the "Print Certificate" function; A4 landscape, 150gsm paper recommended)

### Certificate Information Included

| Field | Content | Editable? |
|-------|---------|----------|
| Programme name | Auto-populated from assessment template | No |
| Participant name | From enrolment record | No (update enrolment before issuing if misspelled) |
| Issue date | Today's date | No |
| Certificate number | Auto-generated (format: NEXARA-[YEAR]-[SEQUENCE]) | No |
| CPD hours | Auto-populated per programme | No |
| Co-brand field | Your organisation name | Yes — set in your trainer profile |

**If a participant's name is misspelled**: Do not issue the certificate; correct the enrolment record first, then re-issue. An incorrectly named certificate cannot be amended post-issue — it must be revoked and reissued.

### Certificate Revocation

If you issue a certificate in error (integrity breach confirmed post-issue; name error not caught in time):
- Contact Nexara L&D at training@nexara.io
- Provide the certificate number
- Nexara will revoke the certificate in the verification system within 2 business days
- Do not attempt to destroy or replace a certificate without Nexara involvement
