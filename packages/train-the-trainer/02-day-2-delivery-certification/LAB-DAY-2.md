# Lab — Day 2: Assessed Delivery

**Programme**: Nexara IMS Train-the-Trainer — Day 2
**Delivery time**: 13:00–15:00 (120 minutes)
**Lab type**: SUMMATIVE ASSESSMENT — formally observed; scored against observer checklist
**Maximum participants**: 8 (one 20-minute slot each; 22 minutes including transition)

---

## Assessment Context

This is a formal assessment. Participants deliver a 20-minute training segment while observed by the Nexara Master Trainer using the `03-trainer-certification/DELIVERY-OBSERVER-CHECKLIST.md`. The rest of the cohort acts as a realistic participant group.

Unlike the Day 1 micro-teaching lab (which was practice and peer feedback only), the Day 2 assessed delivery determines whether the participant achieves the "Nexara Certified Internal Trainer" credential or requires a resubmission.

---

## Scenario Backstory

You are delivering your **first official cohort** as a newly certified Nexara Internal Trainer at Meridian Industrial Group. Today's training audience is:

- **Four operations team members** who are not particularly excited about compliance training and have heard "it's just a new form-filling exercise"
- **Two supervisors** who are engaged and want to understand how the system will help them manage compliance
- **Two senior staff** who already use paper-based equivalents and are worried the digital system will slow them down

Your assessor (the Nexara Master Trainer) is observing today specifically to see how you manage a mixed-motivation group, not just whether you know the content.

This backstory is provided to cohort members acting as participants — they should play their assigned roles authentically for the duration of the 20-minute segment.

## Assessed Delivery Steps

### Step 1 — Final Preparation (12:15–13:00, during lunch)

Participants have lunch break to conduct final preparation. Recommended use:
- Read through the segment once more (do not memorise; be guided by the material)
- Identify one sandbox demonstration and one question to pose to the group
- Time a silent run-through mentally (do not pace the corridor rehearsing aloud — this creates unnecessary stress)
- Review the observer checklist domains one final time

### Step 2 — Room Configuration (12:55–13:00)

Room is set in horseshoe/plenary. "Participant" seats face the delivery space. Observer (Nexara Master Trainer) is seated at the side, out of the direct eyeline of the participant but visible to them.

### Step 3 — Delivery (13:00 onwards, per slot)

**Delivery sequence per participant**:

| Time | Activity |
|------|---------|
| T+0:00 | Observer announces participant name and topic to the group |
| T+0:00 | Participant begins delivery |
| T+18:00 | Observer gives a quiet "2-minute warning" signal (a pre-agreed gesture — e.g. two fingers raised) |
| T+20:00 | Observer says "Time" — participant must stop immediately, even mid-sentence |
| T+20:00–22:00 | Quiet transition; next participant sets up; observer begins domain scoring |
| T+22:00 | Next delivery begins |

**If a participant stops early** (before 20 minutes): the observer does not fill the remaining time. The delivery ends when the participant says "Thank you" or signals completion. The time unused is noted on the checklist — it indicates time management awareness (finishing significantly early is typically a score-reduction indicator on the Programme Management domain).

### Step 4 — Group Participant Roles

Cohort members acting as participants are briefed before the delivery block:

> "Your role for the next two hours is to be genuine participants in a Nexara training session. If your assigned character role is 'sceptical operations worker', be appropriately sceptical — but be realistic, not theatrical. You may ask one naturally-arising question per delivery slot. Do not deliberately derail the trainer. Realistic challenge: yes. Sabotage: no."

**Character assignments** (assigned by trainer during Day 2 briefing):
- Characters 1–2: engaged and willing (ask clarifying questions)
- Characters 3–4: sceptical but present (may question relevance once)
- Characters 5–6: compliant but passive (respond when called on; do not volunteer)
- Characters 7–8: [remaining cohort members in the delivery slot — not playing a role for that slot]

### Step 5 — Observer Scoring

During each delivery, the observer completes the observer checklist:

- Observe the full delivery before scoring any domain
- Note 2–3 specific observable evidence points per domain during delivery
- Score each domain on the 4-point scale after the delivery ends (during the 2-minute transition)
- Do not signal scores to the participant during or immediately after delivery
- Calculate the overall average after all deliveries are complete

## Expected Outcomes

### What a Proficient Delivery Looks Like (Score 3 per domain)

| Domain | Observable at Score 3 |
|--------|---------------------|
| Content Mastery | All content accurate; relevant examples for audience; handles one off-script question competently |
| Facilitation | At least 3 instructional strategies; energy maintained; group engagement actively managed; transitions clear |
| Assessment Admin | Not directly observed in this lab — assessed via Module 4 quiz and written assessment |
| Programme Management | Session is prepared and structured; timing is managed; logistical issues handled without disruption |
| Professionalism | Presentation is professional; communication is inclusive; time management is good |

### Common First-Assessed-Delivery Issues (Not Automatic Failures)

| Issue | Impact | Recovery |
|-------|--------|---------|
| Minor factual inaccuracy | Content domain score 2 | Correct if noticed; don't over-qualify statements |
| Forgetting to ask a question for 15 minutes | Facilitation domain score 2 | Ask before the delivery ends; do not let 20 min pass with no interaction |
| Session ends at 17 minutes (3 minutes early) | Professionalism domain — noted | Use the additional content available in the segment file |
| Participant asks an off-script question and trainer struggles | Content domain score 2 | "Great question — let me come back to that at the end" is acceptable |
| Trainer reads from materials for > 30 seconds continuously | Facilitation domain score 2 | Raise eyes; engage the group before returning to the material |

### What Triggers an Automatic Score of 1 (Insufficient) in a Domain

| Domain | Score-1 Trigger |
|--------|---------------|
| Content Mastery | Material factual error that would actively mislead participants |
| Facilitation | No participant interaction for the full 20 minutes |
| Assessment Admin | N/A for this delivery lab |
| Programme Management | Delivery completely unprepared — no structure, no materials, unable to start |
| Professionalism | Significant co-branding violation; communication that excludes or demeans participants |

## Extension Task (for exceptional deliveries)

If a participant completes a notably strong 20-minute delivery and the observer has no development areas to note, the observer may optionally pose this extension scenario at the debrief (not part of the scored assessment):

> "Your End User cohort of 14 participants includes one participant who is significantly more advanced than the others — they've used a previous version of the system and keep pre-empting your explanations. The rest of the group is starting to disengage because the pace has shifted to the advanced participant. Walk me through how you would handle the next 5 minutes of the session."

This extension tests adaptive facilitation at Bloom's Level 5–6 and is intended purely as a stretch question — it does not affect certification.

---

## Trainer Notes

- Do not adjust scores after delivery based on participant reactions or individual debrief conversations — the checklist score stands as recorded during the delivery
- If two domains are borderline between scores 2 and 3, use the "more evidence than not" principle — score 3 if the majority of evidence supports proficiency
- Ensure you have reviewed and completed all 8 observer checklists before beginning the debrief at 15:45
