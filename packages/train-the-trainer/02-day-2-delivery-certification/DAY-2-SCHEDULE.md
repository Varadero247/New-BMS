## Session Detail

### 08:30 — Recap + Day 2 Overview (30 min)

**Purpose**: Re-establish energy; address knowledge gaps from Day 1 formative check; confirm assessment procedures.

**Energy note**: Day 2 morning typically arrives with higher anxiety than Day 1 — the assessment is today. Open with energy and confidence, not a lengthy recap. Acknowledge the nervousness directly: "Some of you are nervous. Good. It means you care. Let's use it."

**Sequence**:
1. **Energy starter** (3 min) — Quick pair discussion: "What one thing from yesterday will most change how you train?"
2. **Formative check review** (10 min) — Address the two or three questions that generated the most uncertainty from Day 1 (identify these from overnight review of collected forms)
3. **Day 2 agenda walkthrough** (7 min) — Confirm delivery order and times for each participant; display on screen; confirm written assessment timing
4. **Assessment briefing** (10 min) — Explain the observer checklist (display it; walk through the five domains and scoring scale); confirm that both components are required for certification; confirm resubmission pathway

**Transition cue**: "The content this morning will directly help you in this afternoon's delivery. Pay attention to Module 4 especially — you may be asked about assessment delivery in the written assessment."

### 09:00 — Module 4: Assessment Delivery (90 min)

**Materials**: `MODULE-ASSESSMENT-DELIVERY.md`

**Segment breakdown**:
- 09:00–09:20 — Configuring the Training Portal assessment (live demonstration; participants follow along in sandbox)
- 09:20–09:35 — Pre-assessment participant briefing (what to say; how to manage anxiety; invigilation setup)
- 09:35–09:55 — Invigilation requirements (in-person and virtual; integrity rules; what to do if a breach is suspected)
- 09:55–10:10 — Marking, results communication, certificate issuance (live demonstration)
- 10:10–10:25 — Managing failed outcomes (resubmission pathway; communication scripts; empathetic handling)
- 10:25–10:30 — Q&A on assessment processes

**Live mock assessment** (during 09:00–09:20): The trainer runs a 5-minute "mock assessment" — participants experience the participant perspective by completing 5 sample questions on the portal. Then trainer switches roles and demonstrates the admin view (marking, results, certificate issuance).

**Energy note**: This module has high direct relevance — participants know they will be administering real assessments soon. Engagement is typically strong. Lean into the practical demonstration; minimise slide time.

### 13:00 — Assessed Delivery: 20-Minute Observed Segments (120 min)

**Materials**: `LAB-DAY-2.md`, `03-trainer-certification/DELIVERY-OBSERVER-CHECKLIST.md`

**Format**: Each participant delivers a **20-minute training segment** while the Nexara Master Trainer observes using the observer checklist. The rest of the cohort acts as "the participant group" — they are briefed to behave as genuine End User or Module Owner training participants (engaged, occasionally asking a realistic question).

**Delivery order**: Confirmed in Day 2 briefing (yesterday). Post on display.

**Timing per slot**:
- 20 min: Delivery
- 2 min: Grace period (if delivery ends early, trainer calls time at 22:00 regardless)
- Remaining time: quiet transition to next participant (no feedback given during assessment block)

**With 8 participants**: 8 × 22 min = 176 min. Adjust to 15-min slots if necessary (minimum viable assessment window; communicate this change to participants on Day 1 if numbers are confirmed high).

**Energy note**: Observed delivery is the highest-stakes part of the programme. The trainer must remain visibly neutral and focused — no positive or negative facial cues during delivery. Participants are acutely aware of observer reactions.

**Trainer behaviour during assessed delivery**:
- Seated at the side; observer checklist on lap
- Maintain eye contact with the delivery (not the checklist)
- Score as the delivery progresses; do not wait until the end
- Do not intervene unless a serious integrity issue occurs (e.g. participant is reading directly from an answer key)

4. Set up the written assessment on portal or distribute printed papers

### 15:15 — Written Assessment: 20 MCQ, 30 Minutes (30 min)

**Materials**: `ASSESSMENT-WRITTEN.md` (participant-facing)

**Invigilation procedure**:
1. Distribute assessment papers or confirm portal access; do not begin timing until all participants have the paper/screen
2. State: "You have 30 minutes. 20 questions. This is a closed-book assessment. No mobile devices, no reference materials. If you finish early, review your answers before submitting. I will call 'five minutes remaining' at the 25-minute mark. Begin now."
3. Start the timer; note the start time on a piece of paper
4. Move quietly to a position where you can see all participants; do not sit at the front
5. At 25 minutes: "Five minutes remaining."
6. At 30 minutes: "Please stop now. Put down your pen / submit your answer."
7. Collect papers / confirm portal submissions

**Energy note**: Participants will be tired by 15:15. The assessment window is 30 minutes — manageable. Remind them at the start: "This is the final hurdle. You've prepared. Trust your Day 1 and Day 2 notes."

---

### 15:45 — Individual Debrief with Observer Results (45 min)

**Format**: 1:1 conversations with each participant while others review their written assessment papers (returned for personal reference — not marked by trainer; self-marking against answer key distributed at this point).

**Per participant (5–6 minutes)**:
1. "Here is your overall delivery score and your domain breakdown." [hand written checklist]
2. "Your strongest domain was [X] — here's what I observed specifically…" [positive evidence]
3. "The area that most needs development is [Y] — here's what I observed…" [specific, observable]
4. "Your outcome today is [CERTIFIED / CONDITIONAL / FULL RETAKE]." [clear, direct]
5. If CONDITIONAL: "Here's what the resubmission process looks like — you'll receive an email from me tonight confirming the timeline."

**Tone guidance**: This is a professional development conversation, not a performance review. Even participants who fail should leave feeling that the outcome is clear, the path forward is achievable, and that the Nexara trainer respects their effort.

---

### 16:30 — Certificate Ceremony + Resubmission Briefing (30 min)

**Certificate ceremony** (20 min):
- Call each certified participant by name
- Present printed certificate (or announce portal availability)
- Note: do NOT publicly announce who did not pass; simply present to those who did
- Allow participants to photograph certificates

**Resubmission briefing** (5 min, to the full group):
- Explain the resubmission pathway without identifying who needs it: "For anyone who needs a resubmission window, you'll receive individual communication from me by end of today."

**Closing** (5 min):
> "You came in two days ago as experienced professionals. You leave as something more specific: people who understand *how* to teach this content as well as knowing it. For those who are certified today — use it. For those who need one more run at the delivery — it's one session away.
>
> The curriculum packages will be in your inbox by [time]. The portal will update your credential status overnight. Thank you."
