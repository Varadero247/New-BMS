# End User Training Programme — Overview

**Nexara IMS | Version 1.0 | 4 CPD Hours | Two Delivery Formats**

---

## Purpose

The End User Training Programme (EUTP) gives operational staff the task-level skills to use Nexara IMS confidently and correctly in their day-to-day roles. It covers six modules: platform navigation, incident recording, training acknowledgements, permit to work (as a permit holder), observations, and personal reports/dashboards.

No prior system knowledge or technical background is required. This programme is designed to be accessible to all staff regardless of digital literacy level.

---

## Target Audience

| Who | Why they attend |
|-----|----------------|
| Operational staff | Record incidents, near misses, observations |
| All employees | Acknowledge assigned training and procedures |
| Permit holders | Request and operate under Permit to Work |
| Shift workers, field staff, technicians | Log observations; access personal compliance dashboard |
| New joiners | Platform orientation as part of induction |

**No prerequisites**: Participants need only an active Nexara IMS user account with appropriate permissions. Administrator or module owner training is not required.

## Delivery Formats

### Format 1: Virtual Instructor-Led Session

A live, facilitated 4-hour session delivered via Zoom or Microsoft Teams. Participants work through guided exercises in the Nexara sandbox. Recommended for groups of 8–20 participants. See `02-virtual-session/` for full delivery materials.

**Schedule**: 09:00–13:00 with two 10-minute breaks (see VIRTUAL-SESSION-SCHEDULE.md)

### Format 2: Self-Paced E-Learning

Six SCORM-compatible e-learning modules followed by a summative assessment. Participants complete each module at their own pace over any period (typical completion: 4–6 hours including replays). See `03-elearning/` for design specification.

Recommended for:
- Large, geographically distributed organisations
- Shift-working environments where live sessions are impractical
- Asynchronous onboarding programmes

---

## Assessment and Certification

| Criterion | Specification |
|-----------|---------------|
| Format | 20 MCQ |
| Time | 20 minutes (virtual); untimed (e-learning) |
| Pass threshold | ≥ 80% (16/20) — higher than Module Owner (75%) because content is simpler and operational safety is critical |
| Fail | Retake immediately (e-learning) or at next virtual session |
| Certificate | Nexara Platform Foundation — End User Completion Certificate |
| CPD hours | 4 |

**Why 80%?** End user tasks include safety-critical activities (incident recording, permit to work). The higher pass mark ensures participants have a solid understanding of these tasks before operating in a live environment.

---

## Programme Outcomes

On completion, participants will be able to:

1. Log in to Nexara IMS, navigate the dashboard, and find their assigned modules
2. Record an incident or near miss with all required information correctly entered
3. Find and acknowledge assigned training; understand compliance deadline alerts
4. Submit a Permit to Work request and work safely under an approved permit
5. Submit an observation (positive or negative) with appropriate evidence
6. Access their personal compliance dashboard and understand their RAG status

---

## CPD Hours

4 CPD hours are awarded on completion. The End User certificate is appropriate for:
- CPD self-reporting to professional bodies (IOSH, CIEH, NMC, CIPD, etc.)
- Inclusion in personal professional development portfolios
- Evidence of competency for line manager sign-off against job role requirements

---

## Line Manager Involvement

After completing the programme, participants' line managers should complete the **Competency Checklist** (`04-assessments/COMPETENCY-CHECKLIST.md`) to verify that the participant can perform the tasks in a live Nexara environment. The checklist provides on-the-job sign-off and is retained as a competency record.
