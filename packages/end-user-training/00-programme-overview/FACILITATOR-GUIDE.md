# Facilitator Guide — End User Training Programme

---

## Who Is This Audience?

End users are operational staff — the people who work in the field, on the shop floor, in wards, or at desks. They are not IT professionals, not compliance specialists, and not system administrators. Many will be sceptical of "another system to learn" and will need to see immediate personal value to engage fully.

**Your opening mindset**: "This is 4 hours that will save you time every week — because instead of filling in a paper form that disappears into a filing cabinet, you'll record once and it's done."

---

## Tone and Language

| Instead of... | Say... |
|---------------|--------|
| "Navigate to the module" | "Click on [module name] in the left menu" |
| "Complete the mandatory fields" | "These fields have a star — fill them in, they're required" |
| "The system propagates the record" | "Your report is saved and automatically sent to your manager" |
| "Access the compliance matrix" | "See if your training is up to date" |
| "Submit a non-conformance" | "Record what went wrong" |

Use plain language throughout. If you catch yourself using system or compliance jargon, pause and rephrase.

## Inclusivity Guidance

### Digital Literacy Variation

In any group of operational staff, there will be significant variation in digital confidence. Some participants will have used systems like this before; others may be uncomfortable with technology.

**Strategies**:
- Pair more confident participants with less confident ones during exercises (never announce why)
- Slow down for login and navigation — these are often the highest-anxiety moments
- Never ask a participant to demonstrate in front of the group if they haven't confirmed they're confident first
- Normalise difficulty: "If you can't find it, you're not alone — let's all look together"

### English as Additional Language

For participants whose first language is not English:
- Speak more slowly during step-by-step instructions
- Use screen annotations to highlight the exact fields
- Allow extra time during exercises
- Ensure the written lab sheet clearly matches the on-screen steps

### Shift Workers

If delivering to shift workers (e.g., night shift rotating onto days):
- Acknowledge that participants may be tired
- Keep content blocks shorter with more frequent active exercises
- Avoid dense slides — use the live system as the primary teaching tool
- Be flexible about break timing

## Pacing for Both Delivery Formats

### Virtual Instructor-Led (4 hours)

- Modules 1–2 before the first break (09:00–10:20)
- Break at 10:20–10:30
- Modules 3–4 after first break (10:30–11:40)
- Break at 11:40–11:50
- Modules 5–6 after second break (11:50–12:15)
- Assessment + Certificate: 12:15–12:35
- Wrap-up and Q&A: 12:35–13:00

**Energy curve**: Modules 2 and 4 (incident recording and PTW) generate the most engagement — use them as the bookends of each half. Module 6 (reports) can feel anticlimactic — end it with a personal dashboard check: "Look at your own compliance status right now — what colour are you?"

### E-Learning

Pacing is self-determined. The e-learning SCORM package should:
- Enforce sequential module completion (can't skip to Module 4 without completing 1–3)
- Save progress automatically (can be completed across multiple sessions)
- Show estimated completion time on the module index
- Include a progress bar on each module

## Q&A Management

Common end user questions and suggested responses:

| Question | Answer |
|---------|--------|
| "Will my manager see everything I record?" | "Your incident and observation records are visible to your line manager and the HSE/Safety team. Training records are visible to HR and your manager. Your personal dashboard is visible to you and your manager." |
| "What happens if I record something anonymously?" | "Anonymous observations can be submitted without your name attached. Your identity is not visible on the record. However, Nexara administrators (not line managers) can access the submission log — so while your identity is protected from colleagues and immediate management, it is not completely anonymous to the platform." |
| "What if I make a mistake on a record?" | "Most fields can be edited while a record is in draft or open status. Once it's submitted, contact your module owner (e.g. HSE Manager) to make a correction. All changes are logged in the audit trail." |
| "I don't have time to use this system" | "Recording an incident in Nexara takes about 3 minutes. The old paper form took 10 minutes and then had to be re-entered somewhere else. Once it's in the system, it's done — no follow-up unless there's an investigation." |
| "What if the system is down when I need to report an incident?" | "If the system is unavailable: follow your organisation's paper-based emergency reporting procedure, then enter the record into Nexara as soon as access is restored — use the actual incident date, not the date you entered it." |
