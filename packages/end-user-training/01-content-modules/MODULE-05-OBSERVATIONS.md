# Module 5: Observations

**Duration**: 30 minutes | **Format**: Guided exercise

---

## Overview

Observations are your early warning system. They capture unsafe conditions and behaviours before they become incidents — and positive safe behaviours worth recognising. This module shows you how to submit observations, including anonymously.

---

## Part 1: Positive vs Negative Observations (8 min)

### Negative Observations (Unsafe Conditions or Behaviours)

A negative observation is something you've noticed that could cause harm if left uncorrected:

- A trailing cable on a walkway
- A fire exit blocked by equipment
- A colleague not wearing the required PPE
- A chemical container without a label
- A machine guard missing or bypassed

These are not incidents — nothing has happened yet. But they need to be fixed. Recording them in Nexara means the right person is notified and a fix is tracked.

### Positive Observations (Safe Acts or Conditions)

A positive observation recognises something done well:

- A colleague stopping to remind a visitor about PPE requirements
- A work area left exceptionally clean and tidy after a task
- Someone stopping a job because they weren't confident it was safe to proceed

Positive observations are just as important as negative ones. They show management what good looks like and reinforce the behaviours that prevent accidents.

### How This Helps

Every observation submitted is data. Your HSE team uses observation data to:
- Identify where unsafe conditions are recurring (a problem area)
- Spot when a particular behaviour needs a training or procedure update
- Recognise teams with an excellent safety culture

## Part 2: Submitting an Observation (12 min)

### Navigate to Observations

Click **Health & Safety** in the sidebar → click **Observations** → click **New Observation**

### Complete the Form

| Field | What to enter |
|-------|--------------|
| **Observation Type** | Positive (safe) or Negative (unsafe) |
| **Category** | Select from: Housekeeping / PPE Compliance / Equipment / Procedures / Environmental / Ergonomics / Behaviour / Other |
| **Location** | Use the location picker — be as specific as possible |
| **Description** | Describe what you saw — factually, without naming individuals if it's a behaviour observation |
| **Risk Level** (negative only) | Low (minor hazard) / Medium (could cause injury) / High (could cause serious injury) |
| **Photo** | Optional but strongly encouraged — a photo makes the observation much more actionable |
| **Suggested Action** | Optional — if you know what should be done to fix it, suggest it |

### Submitting Anonymous Observations

If your organisation has enabled anonymous submissions:
- Tick **Submit Anonymously** before submitting
- Your name will not appear on the observation record
- The record will show "Anonymous" as the submitter
- You cannot track the status of an anonymous submission after it's been submitted

Note: Anonymous submissions are still received and actioned by the HSE team. If you feel uncomfortable submitting observations with your name, anonymous submission ensures your concerns are still recorded and addressed.

---

## Part 3: Tracking Your Observations (5 min)

For non-anonymous observations, you can track progress:

1. Health & Safety → Observations → My Observations
2. Each observation shows its current status:
   - **Submitted**: Received but not yet reviewed
   - **Under Review**: Being assessed by HSE team
   - **Action Assigned**: A corrective or preventive action has been raised
   - **Closed**: The observation has been addressed (or assessed as low risk)
3. You'll receive a notification when the status changes

If you don't see any action taken within 2 weeks on a Medium or High risk observation, it's appropriate to follow up with your supervisor.
