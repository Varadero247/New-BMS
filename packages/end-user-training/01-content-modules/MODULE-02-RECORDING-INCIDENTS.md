# Module 2: Recording Incidents

**Duration**: 40 minutes | **Format**: Guided exercise

---

## Overview

Reporting incidents promptly and accurately is one of the most important things you can do in Nexara. This module covers: the difference between incidents, near misses, and observations; how to complete an incident report; and what happens after you submit one.

---

## Part 1: Incident vs Near Miss vs Observation (8 min)

Before recording anything, you need to know which record type to use:

| Situation | Record type |
|-----------|------------|
| Someone was injured, equipment was damaged, or a spill occurred | **Incident** |
| Something nearly went wrong but didn't cause harm — "we were lucky" | **Near Miss** |
| You noticed an unsafe condition or behaviour — nothing has happened yet | **Observation** |

**Common confusion**: If a worker trips on a cable and falls, causing an ankle injury — that's an **Incident**. If the worker trips and catches themselves without injury — that's a **Near Miss**. If you just notice the cable on the floor before anyone trips — that's an **Observation**.

**Important**: You should report near misses and observations too. Near misses tell us where the next injury might come from. Organisations that record more near misses have fewer injuries.

## Part 2: How to Record an Incident (15 min)

### Step 1: Navigate to H&S Module

Click **Health & Safety** in the left sidebar → click **Record Incident**

### Step 2: Select the Event Type

- Incident
- Near Miss
- Observation

Select the type that matches what happened.

### Step 3: Fill in the Form

The form has required fields (marked with a red asterisk *) and optional fields. Here's what each required field means:

| Field | What to enter |
|-------|--------------|
| `title` | A short, clear description — "Slip on wet floor near entrance B" not "Accident" |
| `dateOccurred` | The date and time it happened — NOT the date you're reporting it. If you're reporting 2 days later, use the correct date. |
| `severity` | How serious it was. Choose: MINOR (first aid), MODERATE (medical treatment), MAJOR (hospital/significant injury), CRITICAL (life-threatening) |
| `location` | Use the dropdown to pick the site/building/area — don't type it as free text |
| `immediateActions` | What was done right away — first aid given, area cordoned off, supervisor notified, etc. |

### Step 4: Add Details

In the **Description** box: describe what happened in plain language. Be factual — what did you see? What did you hear? What was the sequence of events? You don't need to know why it happened — just record what happened.

### Step 5: Attach Evidence (Optional but Helpful)

If you took photos on your phone:
1. Click **Add Evidence**
2. Click **Upload File** or **Take Photo** (mobile only)
3. Add a brief note describing what the photo shows

Photos are very helpful for investigators. If it's safe to do so and you have your phone, take photos at the scene.

### Step 6: Submit

Click **Submit Report**. You'll see a confirmation screen with your report's reference number (e.g. HS-INC-2026-047). **Take note of this number** — you'll need it if you want to check the status later.

---

## Part 3: What Happens After You Submit (7 min)

Once you submit:

1. **Your HSE Manager is notified** immediately
2. **A reference number is assigned** — you can use this to track progress
3. **An investigation may be opened** — you may be contacted for additional information
4. **You can track status** via Health & Safety → My Reports

Status values you'll see:
| Status | What it means |
|--------|--------------|
| Submitted | Received by the system — not yet reviewed |
| Under Investigation | An investigation has been opened |
| Awaiting Closure | Investigation complete; corrective actions in progress |
| Closed | Investigation and corrective actions complete |

You will receive a notification when the status changes.

---

## Part 4: Common Mistakes (5 min)

**Don't wait to report**: Report as soon as it is safe to do so. Evidence (photos, witness memories) degrades quickly. Many organisations have a mandatory reporting window (e.g. 24 hours for non-serious incidents; immediately for MAJOR or CRITICAL).

**Use the correct date**: The `dateOccurred` field must reflect when the incident happened. If you report 3 days later, change the date to when it actually happened.

**Don't self-diagnose severity**: If you're unsure whether it's MINOR or MODERATE, choose the higher level. Your HSE manager can downgrade it with a note; they cannot upgrade it after the fact without visibility of the original record.

**Report near misses**: Near misses are not failures. Reporting them demonstrates a healthy safety culture. Your report may prevent the next serious injury.
