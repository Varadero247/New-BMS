# Module 4: Permit to Work

**Duration**: 40 minutes | **Format**: Scenario walkthrough

---

## Overview

A Permit to Work (PTW) is a formal safety authorisation for high-risk work. This module explains what requires a permit, how to submit a PTW request in Nexara, how to work safely under a permit, and how to close it when work is complete.

---

## Part 1: What Requires a Permit to Work? (8 min)

Not all work requires a permit. Permits are required for work that carries a significant risk of serious injury if not properly controlled:

| Work Type | Why a Permit Is Required |
|-----------|------------------------|
| **Hot work** (welding, grinding, cutting) | Risk of fire, explosion, toxic fumes |
| **Confined space entry** (tanks, vessels, drains) | Risk of oxygen deficiency, toxic gases, entrapment |
| **Work at height** (above 2 metres) | Risk of fatal fall |
| **Electrical isolation** (working on electrical systems) | Risk of electrocution |
| **Excavation** (ground-breaking >300mm) | Risk of underground services strike, collapse |
| **Chemical handling** (above specified quantities) | Risk of toxic exposure, environmental release |

**If you're unsure**: Ask your supervisor or HSE manager before starting work. It is always safer to check. Starting work without a required permit is a serious safety violation.

## Part 2: Submitting a PTW Request (15 min)

### Step 1: Navigate to Permits

Click **Health & Safety** in the sidebar → click **Permits** → click **New Permit Request**

### Step 2: Select the Permit Type

Choose the type of permit that matches your work. The system loads the correct form and pre-work checklist for that permit type.

### Step 3: Complete the Form

| Field | What to enter |
|-------|--------------|
| **Work Description** | Specific description of the work — "Replace faulty wiring in Junction Box 7, Level 2" not "Electrical work" |
| **Location** | Use the location picker — select the exact area where work will take place |
| **Start Date/Time** | When you plan to start work |
| **Expected End Date/Time** | When you expect to finish (permits expire at this time) |
| **Hazards** | Check all applicable hazards from the list |
| **Control Measures** | For each hazard, describe what you will do to control it |
| **PPE Required** | Check all PPE you will wear |

### Step 4: Complete the Pre-Work Checklist

Each permit type has a mandatory pre-work checklist. Complete every item honestly:
- If an item is **Satisfactory**: tick it
- If an item is **Unsatisfactory**: mark it unsatisfactory and add a note — the permit will record this as a finding

**Do not tick "Satisfactory" if it isn't**: A false pre-work checklist is a serious safety violation that could have legal consequences if someone is injured.

### Step 5: Submit for Approval

Click **Submit for Approval**. The permit is sent to your supervisor or HSE manager for review. You'll receive a notification when it's approved or rejected.

---

## Part 3: Working Under an Approved Permit (10 min)

### Before Starting Work

When your permit is approved:
1. You receive a notification
2. Open the permit: Health & Safety → Permits → My Permits → [Permit Name]
3. **Read the permit conditions carefully** — there may be conditions added by the approver that you must follow
4. Confirm you understand the conditions: click **Confirm Pre-Work Checks**
5. Only then commence work

### During the Work

- The permit is your authority to carry out the work — keep the permit reference handy
- If conditions change (new hazard arises, scope expands), **stop work** and contact your supervisor immediately
- If you have an emergency while under permit: stop work, secure the area, follow your emergency procedure, and contact your HSE manager
- The permit has an expiry time — if you cannot complete within the permitted time, contact your supervisor to discuss extension **before** the permit expires

### After the Work is Complete

1. Navigate to the permit: Health & Safety → Permits → My Permits
2. Confirm all work is complete and the area is safe (no hazards left behind)
3. Click **Close Permit**
4. Complete the closure form: actual finish time, confirmation that the area is safe and clean, any issues encountered
5. Submit closure

**Important**: A permit that expires without formal closure creates a safety audit finding. Always close your permit.

---

## Part 4: Rejected Permits (7 min)

If your PTW is rejected, you'll receive a notification with the rejection reason. Read it carefully:

**Common rejection reasons**:
- Work description too vague — resubmit with more detail
- Hazards not fully identified — add missing hazards
- Control measures insufficient — add or strengthen controls
- Pre-work checklist item marked unsatisfactory — resolve the issue before resubmitting
- Wrong permit type — resubmit using the correct template
- Timing conflict — the area is already under a different permit

Fix the issue and resubmit. If you don't understand the rejection reason, speak with your supervisor before resubmitting.
