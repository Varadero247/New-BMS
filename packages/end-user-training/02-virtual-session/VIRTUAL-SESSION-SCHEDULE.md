## Welcome Block (09:00–09:10) — Facilitator Script

"Good morning and welcome to Nexara IMS End User Training. Over the next four hours you'll learn the six main things you'll be doing in Nexara in your daily role — recording incidents, completing your training, raising permits, and so on. There's no deep technical content today — this is practical and hands-on.

"A few housekeeping points: mute yourselves when you're not speaking; cameras on as much as possible — it helps me understand when people are following along. If you have a question, use the chat or just unmute and ask — there are no silly questions.

"You'll all have received login credentials for the Nexara training sandbox. Please take a moment now to open your browser and go to [training-sandbox-URL]. Try to log in now while I give you a minute."

[1-minute login check]

"Perfect. Today's session is assessed — there's a 20-question quiz at the end. 80% pass mark. Don't worry about it now; if you follow along today, you'll pass easily. Let's start with Module 1 — just getting comfortable in the system."

## Module 2: Incident Recording Exercise Guidance (09:40–10:20)

This is the key hands-on module. Participants need to record a fictional incident in the sandbox.

**Scenario to use**: "It's 14:30. While walking through Warehouse Bay 3, you saw a colleague slip on a puddle of water near the forklift bay entrance. They caught themselves on a railing and weren't injured, but it was very close. You noticed the puddle had been there for at least an hour and came from a leaking pipe overhead."

**Facilitator instructions**:
1. Read the scenario aloud (2 min)
2. Ask: "What type of record should we create?" — Get answers in chat [Expected: Near Miss]
3. Walk through Fields 1-3 together on facilitator's screen (title, date, severity)
4. Say: "Now I want you to complete the rest yourselves on your screen. You have 15 minutes."
5. Circulate via breakout room or chat
6. After 15 min: share facilitator screen showing a completed record; compare with participants' entries
7. Key debrief question: "What's the most important field on this form, and why?" [Expected: dateOccurred — ensures accurate incident timing for investigations]

## Module 4: PTW Scenario Walkthrough Guidance (11:00–11:40)

This module uses a walkthrough approach because PTW is a more complex process.

**Scenario**: "You're a maintenance technician. You need to replace a faulty light fitting 3.5 metres above floor level in the production hall. You'll be using a mobile elevated work platform (MEWP)."

**Walkthrough format**:
- Facilitator demonstrates each step while participants follow on their screen
- Pause at each decision point: "What permit type do we need?" [Work at Height]
- Pause at hazards: "What hazards should we add?" [Working at height, operating MEWP, electrical work]
- Pause at pre-work checklist: "What would you do if you found the MEWP last service was 18 months ago?" [Mark Unsatisfactory; do not proceed; notify supervisor]
- Submit the permit together; observe the Pending Approval status
- Demonstrate: how to check for an approved permit; how to close a permit

## Modules 5+6 Combined Block (11:50–12:15)

**Module 5 (observations)** — 12 min:
- Facilitator demo: submit a negative observation (blocked fire exit)
- Participants: submit their own observation in sandbox (anything they "see" in the fictional environment) — 5 min
- Demo anonymous submission: "Notice the Anonymous checkbox — tick it now and see what changes on the form"

**Module 6 (reports)** — 13 min:
- Facilitator demo: navigate to the personal dashboard and identify RAG status
- Ask in chat: "Is anyone showing amber or red? What training item is causing it?"
- Demo: export training history report as PDF
- Final activity: participants check their personal dashboard status — raise hand (or react) if they're green

## Energy Management Notes

- **09:00**: High energy — capitalise with immediate hands-on login
- **09:40–10:20**: Peak engagement — incident recording exercise is the highlight for most participants
- **10:30**: Post-break restart — use a quick poll: "In your job, how often would you use the H&S module? Daily / Weekly / Monthly / Rarely"
- **11:00**: Training acknowledgements may feel routine — energise with a real example: "Your mandatory fire safety training is in there. If there was a fire tonight and you hadn't done it, could you defend why?"
- **11:40 break**: Two thirds complete — announce "We're nearly there. After the break it's two short modules and then the quiz."
- **12:15**: Assessment — lower energy. Don't rush it. Tell participants: "Take your time. You can change answers before submitting."
