# Knowledge Check Questions — End User Training (Formative, Unscored)

*5 questions per module, 30 questions total. These are formative knowledge checks within the e-learning modules — unscored, instant feedback, serve as self-assessment before the summative.*

---

## Module 1 — Platform Navigation

**M1-Q1.** Where do you click to see your assigned training items on the Nexara dashboard?
- A) The search bar at the top of the screen
- B) The notification bell icon
- C) The Training module in the sidebar navigation ✓
- D) The profile avatar in the top-right corner

**Feedback if wrong**: Your training is in the Training module. Click the graduation cap icon in the left sidebar to access it.

---

**M1-Q2.** You've forgotten your password. What is the quickest way to regain access?
- A) Contact your IT helpdesk immediately
- B) Ask a colleague to share their login
- C) Click "Forgot Password" on the login screen and follow the email reset link ✓
- D) Wait until an admin resets it manually

**Feedback if wrong**: Click "Forgot Password" on the login screen and enter your email address. You'll receive a reset link within 5 minutes.

---

**M1-Q3.** A red number "3" appears on the notification bell. What does this indicate?
- A) 3 system errors have occurred
- B) You have 3 unread notifications waiting for your attention ✓
- C) Your session will expire in 3 minutes
- D) 3 colleagues have sent you messages

**Feedback if wrong**: The number on the notification bell shows unread notifications — things that need your attention, like assigned training or permit updates.

---

**M1-Q4.** Which notification type requires the most urgent action?
- A) A new profile photo has been uploaded
- B) Training has been assigned to you
- C) Training is overdue — deadline has passed ✓
- D) A colleague submitted an observation

**Feedback if wrong**: Overdue training means a deadline has already passed. This requires immediate action.

---

**M1-Q5.** Where in Nexara would you change your notification preferences from in-app only to email and in-app?
- A) Settings → Organisation Settings
- B) Profile avatar → Profile Settings → Notification Preferences ✓
- C) Training → Course Settings
- D) Health & Safety → My Profile

**Feedback if wrong**: Your personal notification preferences are in your Profile Settings — click your profile avatar (top-right) then Profile Settings.

## Module 2 — Recording Incidents

**M2-Q1.** You witness a spill from an overheating machine that creates a smoke hazard but doesn't injure anyone. Which record type should you create?
- A) Incident
- B) Near Miss ✓
- C) Observation
- D) No record needed — nothing was damaged

**Feedback if wrong**: A near miss — an unplanned event that had the potential to cause harm but didn't. This definitely needed recording.

---

**M2-Q2.** You're recording an incident that happened yesterday. What date should you enter in the `dateOccurred` field?
- A) Today's date — when you're creating the record
- B) The date the investigation is opened
- C) Yesterday's date — when the incident actually happened ✓
- D) The date your supervisor was notified

**Feedback if wrong**: `dateOccurred` must be when the incident happened, not when you're reporting it. Accurate dating is critical for investigations.

---

**M2-Q3.** A colleague slipped and required hospital treatment with suspected fractures. Which severity level applies?
- A) MINOR
- B) MODERATE
- C) MAJOR ✓
- D) CRITICAL

**Feedback if wrong**: MAJOR = hospitalisation, significant injury, lost time. CRITICAL would be a life-threatening injury.

---

**M2-Q4.** After submitting an incident report, where can you check its current status?
- A) Email inbox — you'll receive a daily status update
- B) Health & Safety → My Reports → select the incident ✓
- C) Ask your supervisor — they receive all updates
- D) You cannot track the status — only HSE managers can see records

**Feedback if wrong**: Navigate to Health & Safety → My Reports to see the status of all your submissions.

---

**M2-Q5.** Why is it important to report near misses, even if no one was hurt?
- A) It is a legal requirement to report all near misses regardless of outcome
- B) Near misses indicate where the next injury could occur — reporting them helps prevent future harm ✓
- C) Near miss data is used to calculate TRIR statistics only
- D) Reporting near misses is optional and only required for contractors

**Feedback if wrong**: Near misses are leading indicators of future injuries. Organisations that report more near misses proactively prevent serious incidents.

## Module 3 — Training Acknowledgements

**M3-Q1.** Your training dashboard shows one item in red. What does this mean?
- A) The course is not available in your language
- B) A mandatory training item has passed its completion deadline ✓
- C) The course has been cancelled
- D) The course requires manager approval before you can access it

**Feedback if wrong**: Red = overdue. The completion deadline has passed. Complete it immediately and notify your line manager.

---

**M3-Q2.** You completed a First Aid certificate externally last week. How do you record this in Nexara?
- A) Ask your manager to update your training record on your behalf
- B) Training → find the course → Record External Completion → enter date, result, upload certificate ✓
- C) Email the certificate to HR — they will update the system
- D) External training does not need to be recorded in Nexara

**Feedback if wrong**: You can record your own external training completions. Go to the course in your training list, click Record External Completion, and upload your certificate.

---

**M3-Q3.** A mandatory training item shows status "Due Soon" in amber. When is it likely due?
- A) In the next 7 days
- B) Within the next 30 days ✓
- C) In the next 90 days
- D) It has already expired

**Feedback if wrong**: Amber "Due Soon" items are due within 30 days. Complete them this week to maintain your green status.

---

**M3-Q4.** You fail a knowledge check on the first attempt. What should you do?
- A) Contact your training manager — you'll need a manual exemption to proceed
- B) Review the feedback showing which answers were incorrect, then retry ✓
- C) Report a system error — knowledge checks should not have a pass mark
- D) Move on to the next module and come back later

**Feedback if wrong**: Review the feedback after a failed attempt — it shows which answers were incorrect and explains why. Then retry.

---

**M3-Q5.** How many days before a training deadline does Nexara automatically notify your line manager?
- A) 30 days
- B) 14 days
- C) 7 days ✓
- D) 1 day

## Module 4 — Permit to Work

**M4-Q1.** Which of the following work activities ALWAYS requires a Permit to Work in Nexara's standard configuration?
- A) Routine office cleaning
- B) Replacing a light bulb at desk height
- C) Welding on a live production line ✓
- D) Walking through a warehouse

**Feedback if wrong**: Hot work (welding) on a production line requires a Hot Work Permit. The other activities don't meet any PTW trigger condition.

---

**M4-Q2.** During the PTW pre-work checklist, you find that the fall arrest equipment was last inspected 18 months ago (requirement: annual). What should you do?
- A) Mark it as Satisfactory and proceed — minor delays are acceptable
- B) Mark it as Unsatisfactory, note the issue, stop work, and notify your supervisor ✓
- C) Complete the work quickly before anyone notices
- D) Cross out the checklist item and proceed

**Feedback if wrong**: Never tick Satisfactory when it isn't. Mark Unsatisfactory, notify your supervisor, and do not start work until the issue is resolved.

---

**M4-Q3.** Your Work at Height permit is approved for 08:00–16:00. At 15:45, you have almost finished but need another 30 minutes. What should you do?
- A) Continue working — finishing 30 minutes late is a minor variance
- B) Stop work at 16:00, secure the area, and reapply for a new permit ✓
- C) Ask a colleague to take over the work after the permit expires
- D) Change the permit end time yourself in the system

**Feedback if wrong**: A permit expires at its stated end time. Stop, secure the area, then reapply for a new permit. Never continue work under an expired permit.

---

**M4-Q4.** After completing permitted work, you close the permit in Nexara. What must you confirm in the closure form?
- A) Your supervisor has observed the completed work
- B) The work is complete, the area is safe, and any materials are removed ✓
- C) The permit approver has signed the physical paper permit
- D) A new permit for the next task has been submitted

**Feedback if wrong**: The closure form requires confirmation that work is complete, the area is safe, and there are no residual hazards — protecting the next person to work in that area.

---

**M4-Q5.** Your PTW is rejected with the note: "Hazards not fully identified." What is the correct next step?
- A) Escalate to your manager — rejection decisions can be appealed
- B) Read the rejection note, add the missing hazards and controls, and resubmit ✓
- C) Submit the permit to a different approver who may be more lenient
- D) Proceed without the permit — you've done this work safely before

**Feedback if wrong**: Read the rejection reason carefully, address the identified gap, and resubmit. The approver is protecting you.

## Module 5 — Observations

**M5-Q1.** You see a colleague wearing gloves when the PPE requirement for their task is safety glasses only. This is:
- A) A negative observation (unsafe condition)
- B) Not worth reporting — it's minor
- C) A positive or negative observation depending on whether glasses are also worn ✓
- D) An incident (PPE non-compliance)

**Feedback if wrong**: If glasses are also worn: positive observation (extra PPE, no issue). If glasses are NOT worn: negative observation (wrong PPE). Context matters.

---

**M5-Q2.** Which risk level should you assign to an observation where a fire exit is blocked by a delivery pallet?
- A) Low
- B) Medium ✓
- C) High
- D) Critical — call the emergency services immediately

**Feedback if wrong**: Medium — a blocked fire exit poses a risk of injury in an emergency but doesn't create immediate harm in normal conditions. If a fire was active: call for evacuation immediately; record later.

---

**M5-Q3.** You notice unsafe behaviour by a colleague and want to report it without being identified. How do you submit anonymously?
- A) Create a fake account to submit the observation
- B) Tick the "Submit Anonymously" checkbox before submitting ✓
- C) Ask another colleague to submit on your behalf
- D) Anonymous submissions are not available in Nexara

**Feedback if wrong**: Tick "Submit Anonymously" on the observation form. Your name will not appear on the record.

---

**M5-Q4.** You submit an observation about a leaking pipe. Two weeks later it's still showing "Submitted" status. What should you do?
- A) Nothing — the process takes time
- B) Submit the same observation again
- C) Follow up with your supervisor or HSE manager ✓
- D) Escalate to SUPER_ADMIN

**Feedback if wrong**: If a Medium or High risk observation has not been actioned within 2 weeks, it's appropriate to follow up with your supervisor.

---

**M5-Q5.** Why does Nexara ask you to add a photo to observations?
- A) Photos are required by law for all safety observations
- B) Photos help the HSE team prioritise and fix issues faster, and provide evidence that the condition existed ✓
- C) Photos are only useful if the observation is anonymous
- D) The system requires at least one attachment on every record

**Feedback if wrong**: Photos make observations much more actionable. The HSE team can see exactly what needs fixing and provide clear instructions for remediation.

- A) My Observations report
- B) My Incident Records report
- C) My Training History report ✓
- D) My Permits report

**Feedback if wrong**: Training → Reports → My Training History shows all completions with dates and results.

---

**M6-Q4.** A colleague tells you they've completed the online training but their status is still showing red in Nexara. What is most likely the cause?
- A) The system is experiencing a technical fault
- B) The training completion was not submitted in the system — clicking through the course does not record completion unless you click "Complete Course" or submit the knowledge check ✓
- C) Their account has been locked
- D) The training course has expired

**Feedback if wrong**: Completion is only recorded when the participant explicitly submits ("Complete Course" button or passes the knowledge check). Watching/reading alone does not mark it complete.

---

**M6-Q5.** You receive an automated notification that your Moving and Handling certificate expires in 28 days. What is the correct response?
- A) Ignore it — 28 days is plenty of time
- B) Contact HR immediately — they need to re-issue the certificate
- C) Book and complete a refresher course before the expiry date; record the new certificate in Nexara ✓
- D) Ask your line manager to extend the validity period

**Feedback if wrong**: You need to complete a refresher course and record the new certificate before the expiry date to maintain your green status.
